# Plan: Adopt "Show, Don't Tell" Copy Policy

## Problem

Every interior page (`/learn`, `/practice`, `/words`, `/alphabet`, `/auth`) opens with a full `page-intro` hero block — eyebrow, oversized h1, body paragraph, and decorative badges — that narrates what the page does before the page actually does it. The homepage compounds this with redundant value-prop bullet lists, architecture explainer paragraphs, and a "How it works" lead that restates its own step cards.

This violates the "show, don't tell" principle: the interactive UI already communicates each page's purpose. The intro blocks add visual noise, push the actual content down, and treat returning learners like first-time visitors on every visit.

---

### 1. `/learn` — `src/routes/learn/+page.svelte:69–81`

Current `page-intro` block:

- Eyebrow: "Thai reading path"
- H1: "Start with a whole word, then peel it open."
- Body: "Each lesson starts with a real Thai word you could meet on a sign, menu, or appliance, then walks you inward through syllables, letters, rules, and a quick drill loop."
- 3 badges: "short lessons", "real words first", "local progress"

**Issue:** The lesson cards already show the anchor word, stage number, new letters, and completion status. A returning learner knows what a lesson is. A new learner finds out by doing lesson 1. The entire block is a marketing pitch that the card grid renders unnecessary.

## Remediation Plan

### Phase 1: Remove `page-intro` blocks from interior pages

These pages serve returning learners who already know what the page does. Replace each `page-intro` with a minimal, scannable page header — just an h1, optionally with a short subtitle or inline badge. The actual content below becomes the first thing the user sees.

#### `/learn` — Lesson Index

Replace the full `page-intro` (lines 69–81) with a compact page header:

```svelte
<header class="learn__header">
 <h1>Thai reading path</h1>
 <span class="badge badge--primary">{lessons.length} lessons</span>
</header>
```

- Move the lesson count badge inline with the heading (useful quick stat).
- Drop the eyebrow, body paragraph, and the other two badges ("real words first", "local progress") — these are marketing copy, not navigation aids.
- Remove the `.page-intro` section and its `&__intro` style rule; add a simple flex header style.

#### `/practice` — Practice

Remove the `page-intro` block entirely (lines 93–105). The start screen card (lines 123–143) already contains:

- An h2: "Practice what already feels familiar."
- The available drill count
- Metric pills for known letters / words
- The "Start Practice Session" CTA

This card can serve as the page header. Optionally promote its h2 to h1 for the page, or add a bare h1 before the conditional blocks:

```svelte
<h1 class="sr-only">Practice</h1>
```

The `<title>` and meta description already communicate the page purpose to search and screen readers. Remove `&__intro` style rule.

#### `/words` — Known Words

Replace the `page-intro` (lines 26–33) with a simple header:

```svelte
<header class="words__header">
 <h1>Known Words</h1>
</header>
```

The word grid or empty state follows immediately. No subtitle needed — the cards are self-documenting. Remove `&__intro` style rule.

#### `/alphabet` — Alphabet

Replace the `page-intro` (lines 78–89) with a compact header that keeps the useful stats:

```svelte
<header class="alphabet__header">
 <h1>Alphabet</h1>
 <span class="badge badge--primary">{$knownLetters.length} of {allLetters.length} unlocked</span>
</header>
```

The progress bar and tile grids follow immediately. The "X of Y unlocked" badge is more useful than the old "X unlocked" + "Y taught so far" pair because it communicates progress as a ratio. Remove `&__intro` style rule.

#### `/auth` — Auth

Remove the `page-intro` block entirely (lines 84–107). The auth panel card already has an h1 and contextual copy in each state:

- Signed in: "Account" + email
- Not configured: "Sign in" + "Authentication is not configured"
- Code flow: "Sign in" + "Use a one-time code to keep your progress synced"

Replace the verbose summary card paragraphs with operational facts only:

- Sign-in email card: Drop "You are using Glyphin's email code sign-in flow." (obvious from the UI).
- Sync status card: Keep the last-refreshed timestamp. Drop "We refresh your learner projection after sign-in and when progress sync runs." (implementation detail).
- Synced lessons card: Keep the count. Replace the conditional text with a simple resume link or "Resume lesson N" if available.
- Bottom meta paragraph: Drop entirely. Replace with a subtle hint if needed, e.g. a small `<small>` line: "Local progress stays instant. Signing in adds cross-device sync."

Remove `&__intro` style rule.

### Phase 2: Trim homepage redundancy

#### GuestHomeShell — Remove value-prop bullet list

The hero lead paragraph ("Glyphin teaches script and vocabulary together…") already communicates the concept. The 3 bullet points below it restate the same idea in slightly different words. Remove the `ul.guest-home__points` and its styles entirely.

Optionally shorten the lead paragraph to one sentence:

> "Glyphin teaches script and vocabulary together, so your first lesson already feels like reading the real thing."

can become:

> "Script and vocabulary taught together — your first lesson already reads like the real thing."

Drop the "Choose your path" lead paragraph (lines 73–76). The h2 "Choose your path" is sufficient.

#### LearnerHomeHub — Shorten lead, remove aside redundancy

Shorten the lead paragraph. Replace the auth sync explanation with a small inline hint:

```svelte
<p class="learner-home__lead">
 {completionCopy}
 {#if authenticated}
  <small><a href="/auth">Sync settings</a></small>
 {:else}
  <small><a href="/auth">Save progress across devices</a></small>
 {/if}
</p>
```

Remove or simplify the aside card paragraph ("Jump straight back into the next reading loop…"). Keep the h2 and "Browse lesson map" link only.

#### HowItWorksSection — Remove lead paragraph

Remove the `p.method__lead` and its styles. The 3 step cards carry the entire message. Keep the h2 "How it works" as a section heading.

### Phase 3: Clean up shared styles

- Remove the `.page-intro` block from `src/lib/styles/global.scss:279–319` once no page references it.
- Remove all per-page `&__intro` style rules (already noted per-page above).
- Scan for any remaining `page-intro` class references and remove them.

### Phase 4: Document the policy

Add a "Copy and Page Design" section to `src/routes/AGENTS.md`:

```markdown
## Copy and Page Design

- **Show, don't tell.** Let the interactive UI communicate what a page does. Do not add a page-intro hero block that narrates the content before the content appears.
- **Page headers** should be a concise h1 (and optionally one inline badge or short subtitle). No eyebrow, body paragraph, or decorative badge row.
- **First-time visitor context** belongs on the homepage (landing section, How it works). Interior pages serve returning learners who already know the purpose.
- **Operational hints** (sync status, prerequisites) belong in the relevant UI control, tooltip, or aside — not in a page-level paragraph.
- **Architecture explanations** ("learner projection", "local-first", "canonical") are implementation details. Never surface them in learner-facing copy.
```

Also update the top-level `AGENTS.md` "Quality Bar" section with a one-line reference:

> Keep page-level copy minimal. Let the UI speak for itself; see route rules for the "show, don't tell" policy.
