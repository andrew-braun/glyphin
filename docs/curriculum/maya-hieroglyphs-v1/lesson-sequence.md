# Maya Hieroglyphs Reading Lesson Sequence

This staged outline is a review-ready starting point for Maya glyph-block
recognition through sign labels, calendar signs, and transliteration. It does
not attempt to ship inscription drawings or complete epigraphic readings.

## Course Shape

- First-session reading win: recognize `AJAW`, `K'IN`, and dot/bar numerals in
  reviewed museum-style contexts.
- Approximate lesson count: 12 lessons plus optional calendar and place-name
  review.
- Structural family: logosyllabic historic script with block layout, logograms,
  syllabograms, affixes, calendar notation, and major sign variants.
- Required app support beyond the current Thai runtime: image or catalog-sign
  display, glyph-block order metadata, sign variant IDs, transliteration fields,
  mixed-direction layout notes, and Mayanist review gates.

## Stage 1: First Glyph-Block Wins

| Lesson | Anchor       | New units     | Rule or pattern                                      | Review units | Drill focus                                      |
| ------ | ------------ | ------------- | ---------------------------------------------------- | ------------ | ------------------------------------------------ |
| 1      | `AJAW`       | AJAW-logogram | A glyph sign can represent a reviewed word or title. | none         | Match a title label to its reviewed sign family. |
| 2      | `K'IN`       | K'IN-logogram | Calendar and name signs can share visible forms.     | AJAW         | Recognize a high-salience day or sun sign.       |
| 3      | dot numerals | numeral-dot   | Dots count one through four in Mayan-style numerals. | K'IN         | Count simple date components.                    |
| 4      | bar numerals | numeral-bar   | Bars count fives and combine with dots.              | numeral-dot  | Read small date numbers in museum labels.        |

## Stage 2: Semantic Signs And Titles

| Lesson | Anchor | New units  | Rule or pattern                                        | Review units | Drill focus                                        |
| ------ | ------ | ---------- | ------------------------------------------------------ | ------------ | -------------------------------------------------- |
| 5      | `HA'`  | HA'-water  | Concrete logograms can be taught before full grammar.  | AJAW         | Match semantic sign labels and glosses.            |
| 6      | `CHAN` | CHAN-sky   | Sign readings need catalog and context review.         | K'IN         | Contrast sky and sun/day signs.                    |
| 7      | `KAB'` | KAB'-earth | Semantic pairs support memory but still need variants. | CHAN         | Sort earth and sky anchors.                        |
| 8      | `YAX`  | YAX-sign   | One sign label can carry a broad semantic range.       | AJAW, K'IN   | Read reviewed royal or calendar labels with notes. |

## Stage 3: Block Layout And Inscription Domains

| Lesson | Anchor    | New units            | Rule or pattern                                             | Review units             | Drill focus                                              |
| ------ | --------- | -------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------ | -------------------------------------------------------- |
| 9      | `WITZ`    | WITZ-mountain        | Place and landscape signs appear in stela contexts.         | KAB'                     | Recognize landscape signs without copying monument text. |

## Coverage Notes

- Target-domain samples: museum stela labels, calendar-date diagrams, royal name
  discussions, inscription transliterations, and sign catalog workbenches.
- Known-unit coverage goal: by Lesson 8, learners can recognize title, day,
  numeral, sky, earth, water, and first/green sign labels.
- Known-anchor coverage goal: learners should read a small set of reviewed sign
  labels and calendar components without assuming complete decipherment skill.
- Deferred high-load material: full glyph drawings, phonetic complements,
  positional variants, dynastic history, actual monument readings, and licensing
  of inscription images.
