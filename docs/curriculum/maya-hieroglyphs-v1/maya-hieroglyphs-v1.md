# Maya Hieroglyphs Reading Curriculum Notes

This durable course note records the v1 boundary and bootstrap decisions for a
Maya hieroglyphs reading course.

## Course Boundary

- Language/script: Classic Maya and related Mayan-language inscriptional signs
  represented through reviewed sign labels, `myn-Maya`, script `Maya`.
- Target learner: museum visitor, Mesoamerican-history learner, or epigraphy
  beginner who wants to recognize a small set of glyph signs and labels.
- Target domains: museum stela labels, calendar dates, royal names and titles,
  inscription transliterations, and sign catalog workbenches.
- In scope for v1: sign-label recognition, dot/bar numerals, selected logograms,
  simple syllabogram awareness, glyph-block reading order caveats, and reviewed
  calendar terms.
- Out of scope for v1: complete decipherment, copying monument text, shipping
  inscription drawings, dynastic history as lesson content, and full sign catalog
  coverage.

## Sequencing Rationale

- Frequency sources: Unicode for Mayan numeral metadata, CMHI and specialist
  databases for discovery, Mesoweb and FAMSI for background research, and
  app-authored examples for shipped content.
- First-session decoding target: `AJAW`, `K'IN`, and dot/bar numerals, because
  they are common museum-facing concepts and make the block-layout constraint
  visible early.
- Stage 1 goal: show learners that Maya glyph reading is curated sign and block
  recognition rather than normal left-to-right text.
- Stage 2 goal: add concrete semantic signs with strong cultural review.
- Stage 3 goal: introduce calendar and place-name contexts after the app can
  represent blocks and variants.

## Script Notes

- Maya hieroglyphic text needs block layout and sign-variant metadata. Plain
  Unicode text is not enough for the full script.
- Direction is mixed because reading order depends on glyph-block arrangement
  and inscription layout.
- Corpus scarcity, drawing rights, and catalog identifiers are central risks.
- Any learner-visible sign drawing or image requires explicit rights review.

## Validation Notes

- Segmentation review: verify sign labels, block order, variant IDs, and
  calendar components with a Mayanist reviewer.
- Pronunciation review: use transliteration and sign labels unless a reviewer
  approves specific phonological hints.
- Cultural review: avoid presenting sacred, royal, or community-linked material
  as generic puzzle content.
- License review: keep external drawings, inscription text, and catalog data out
  of shipped content until permissions are explicit.
