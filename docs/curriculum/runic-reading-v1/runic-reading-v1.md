# Runic Reading Curriculum Notes

This durable course note records the v1 boundary and bootstrap decisions for a
Runic reading course.

## Course Boundary

- Language/script: undetermined language content in the Runic script,
  `und-Runr`, ISO 15924 `Runr`.
- Target learner: museum visitor, historical-script hobbyist, or beginner who
  wants to recognize runes and transliteration labels before studying a specific
  Germanic language.
- Target domains: futhark charts, museum inscriptions, runestone labels,
  academic transliterations, and replica artifact captions.
- In scope for v1: an Elder Futhark first pass, rune-shape recognition,
  transliteration labels, selected rune names, and period caveats.
- Out of scope for v1: full Old Norse or Proto-Germanic language instruction,
  Younger Futhark as default, Anglo-Saxon futhorc as default, magical claims,
  bind-rune production, and unreviewed modern symbolic uses.

## Sequencing Rationale

- Frequency sources: Unicode metadata for inventory, inscription databases for
  discovery-only attestation checks, rune-name references for sequencing,
  Junicode for rendering checks, and app-authored examples for shipped content.
- First-session decoding target: `ᚠᚢᚦᚨᚱᚲ`, because it teaches the script's own
  sequence and unlocks immediate chart recognition.
- Stage 1 goal: establish the first futhark runes and the idea of
  transliteration labels.
- Stage 2 goal: add high-payoff runes through rune-name anchors while marking
  period and language uncertainty.
- Stage 3 goal: introduce longer rune-name chunks, variant-shape review, and
  sensitivity notes for modern contexts.

## Script Notes

- Runic is not one language. A v1 course must label tradition, period, and
  transliteration choices whenever possible.
- Digital examples can be left-to-right for consistency, but inscriptions may
  vary in direction, separator use, orientation, and local convention.
- Rune names are useful teaching anchors, not direct evidence for every
  inscriptional reading.
- Pronunciation hints are reconstructed or conventional and should never be
  presented as modern native speech.

## Validation Notes

- Segmentation review: verify rune separators and variant forms are not flattened
  into ordinary Latin punctuation or one undifferentiated rune value.
- Pronunciation review: use a Runology or historical linguistics reviewer for
  transliteration labels and period notes.
- Cultural review: flag sacred, mythological, and modern extremist misuse risks
  before learner-facing copy is written.
- License review: keep inscription database text and transcriptions out of
  shipped content until reuse and attribution terms are approved.
