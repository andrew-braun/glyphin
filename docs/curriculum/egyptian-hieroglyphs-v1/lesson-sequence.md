# Egyptian Hieroglyphs Reading Lesson Sequence

This staged outline is a review-ready starting point, not final learner-facing
lesson copy. It targets museum and inscription literacy rather than modern
Egyptian Arabic or spoken reconstruction.

## Course Shape

- First-session reading win: recognize `𓋹`, `𓇳`, and `𓉐` as real museum
  reading targets with cautious transliteration labels.
- Approximate lesson count: 10 core lessons plus optional specialist-reviewed
  expansion for quadrat layout and names.
- Structural family: logographic and phonographic writing with determinatives,
  sign-list identities, mixed orientation, and Egyptological transliteration.
- Required app support beyond the current Thai runtime: script-neutral text
  fields, sign IDs, transliteration display, right-to-left and column metadata,
  format-control preservation, and nonmodern pronunciation labels.

## Stage 1: First Decoding Wins

| Lesson | Anchor | New units      | Rule or pattern                                           | Review units | Drill focus                                                   |
| ------ | ------ | -------------- | --------------------------------------------------------- | ------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1      | `𓋹`    | ankh sign      | A sign can be a whole-word reading target.                | None         | Match sign to reviewed gloss without implying speech fluency. |
| 2      | `𓇳`    | sun or Ra sign | Museum labels may show a logogram and a transliteration.  | `𓋹`          | Distinguish sign meaning from transliteration value.          |
| 3      | `𓉐`    | pr house sign  | Some signs can be read as a word and as a phonetic value. | `𓋹`, `𓇳`     | Read object-shaped signs and choose conservative glosses.     |

## Coverage Notes

- Target-domain samples: museum labels, artifact captions, sign-list entries,
  cartouche examples, beginner Egyptology readers, and inscription excerpts.
- Known-grapheme or known-unit coverage goal: by Lesson 7, learners know several
  iconic logograms plus common uniliteral signs used in controlled examples.
- Known-word or known-anchor coverage goal: early learners can identify a small
  set of signs they are likely to see in museums without overclaiming fluency.
- Deferred high-load material: full Gardiner inventory, quadrat typography,
  determinative classes, grammar, royal titulary, full cartouche reading,
  unencoded variants, and pronunciation reconstruction.
