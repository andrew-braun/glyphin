# Syriac Reading Lesson Sequence

This staged outline is a review-ready starting point for Syriac-script reading.
It is not final learner-facing copy and it assumes specialist review before any
pronunciation or cultural note is shipped.

## Course Shape

- First-session reading win: read `ܡܝܐ`, `ܫܠܡܐ`, and `ܠܚܡܐ` as short useful
  words.
- Approximate lesson count: 12 core lessons plus optional vocalization and
  cultural-site review.
- Structural family: RTL joining abjad with matres lectionis, optional vowel
  points, dot distinctions, and contextual shaping.
- Required app support beyond the current Thai runtime: language-agnostic text
  fields, RTL/bidi isolation, joining-script font QA, optional combining marks,
  and script-specific drill styling.

## Optional Review

- Add vowel-point reading only after unpointed letter recognition is stable.
- Add denomination, dialect, and liturgical-register notes only with specialist
  review.
- Add image-backed signage tasks only after font shaping and bidi QA are done.

## Coverage Notes

- Target-domain samples: community labels, church and cultural-site signage,
  classroom primers, simple food words, water labels, and short greetings.
- Known-unit coverage goal: by Lesson 4, learners can read short joined words
  with alap, mim, yodh, lamadh, shin, beth, and taw.
- Known-word coverage goal: by Lesson 8, learners can recognize water, peace,
  bread, house, fish, milk, market, and book.
- Deferred high-load material: full dialect coverage, full vowel pointing,
  pronunciation detail, manuscript hands, Estrangela versus Serto presentation,
  and copied third-party text.
