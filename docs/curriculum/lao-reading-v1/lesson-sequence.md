# Lao Reading Lesson Sequence

This staged outline is a review-ready starting point, not final learner-facing
lesson copy. It targets practical Lao reading before full consonant-class or
tone-rule mastery.

## Course Shape

- First-session reading win: read `ນ້ຳ`, `ກາເຟ`, and `ລາຄາ` in survival, cafe,
  and market contexts.
- Approximate lesson count: 15 core lessons plus late public-facility review.
- Structural family: Lao abugida with consonant classes, dependent vowels before,
  above, below, after, or around bases, tone marks, final consonants, phrase-level
  spaces, and syllable-frame decoding.
- Required app support beyond the current Thai runtime: language-agnostic text
  fields, dictionary segmentation, syllable-frame metadata, vowel-position
  metadata, tone-mark metadata, script-aware fonts, and course-aware progress
  state.

| 2      | `ກາເຟ` | ກ, າ, ເ, ຟ | Preposed vowels are seen before the consonant but read with it.  | ນ            | Decode a familiar cafe word by visual scan order.    |

## Coverage Notes

- Target-domain samples: cafe boards, food menus, market price signs, shops,
  vehicle or bus signs, road labels, open and closed signs, hotel signs, and
  bathroom signs.
- Known-grapheme or known-unit coverage goal: by Lesson 4, cover common bases,
  long-aa, first preposed vowel, and tone marks; by Lesson 12, add final
  consonants, open and closed signs, and common syllable frames.
- Known-word or known-anchor coverage goal: learner reads at least six practical
  menu, price, shop, and transit words by Lesson 8 and a compact wayfinding set
  by Lesson 14.
- Deferred high-load material: full consonant-class matrix, full tone rules,
  Lao digits, rare letters, complete vowel chart, Pali and Sanskrit spelling,
  handwriting, production spelling, and grammar.
