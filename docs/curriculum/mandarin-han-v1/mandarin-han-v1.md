# Mandarin Han Reading Curriculum Notes

Mandarin Han Reading v1 is a simplified-first reading course for menus, signage,
and short public text. It teaches characters and components for recognition, not
handwriting or full Chinese literacy.

## Course Boundary

- Language/script: Mandarin Chinese in Simplified Han, `zh-Hans`, ISO 15924
  `Hans`.
- Target learner: English-speaking traveler or beginner seeking quick decoding
  of real-world Mandarin text.
- Target domains: restaurant menus, food labels, transit signage, public notices,
  and retail signage.
- In scope for v1: 25 to 35 high-payoff simplified characters, component hints,
  pinyin support, manually segmented words, and traditional-variant metadata.
- Out of scope for v1: handwriting, stroke order, calligraphy, Cantonese,
  production writing, grammar beyond isolated words, tone assessment, and broad
  HSK coverage.

## Sequencing Rationale

- Start with food and drink because menus provide fast real-world payoff.
- Teach simple component patterns such as food, water, mouth, money, and doorway
  as recognition hints rather than a formal radical course.
- Add signage pairs after menu foundations so learners can practice binary and
  directional contrasts.
- Keep pinyin visible as support, but make Han recognition the main task.

## Validation Notes

- Segmentation review: manually segment all v1 words and do not rely on raw
  tokenizer output for learner-visible content.
- Pronunciation review: native-speaker review is required for pinyin, tone marks,
  and simplified/traditional variant notes.
- Cultural review: confirm that menu and sign examples are natural in Mainland
  Mandarin contexts.
- License review: keep CEDICT, jieba, HSK, CLDR, and corpus-derived content out
  of shipped examples until attribution obligations are approved.
