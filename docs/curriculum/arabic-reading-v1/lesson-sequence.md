# Arabic Script Reading Lesson Sequence

This staged outline is a review-ready starting point, not final learner-facing
lesson copy. It targets practical Arabic-script reading in Modern Standard Arabic
and region-neutral travel contexts before full grammar or dialect study.

## Course Shape

- First-session reading win: read `لا`, `باب`, `ماء`, and `شاي` as real signs or
  menu words.
- Approximate lesson count: 16 core lessons plus optional sign-pair and
  facility-word consolidation.
- Structural family: right-to-left abjad with joining behavior, positional glyph
  forms, long-vowel letters, optional short-vowel marks, hamza and alif variants,
  and lam-alif shaping.
- Required app support beyond the current Thai runtime: RTL rendering, bidi
  isolation for mixed-script UI, language-agnostic text fields, joining-form
  metadata, optional diacritic display, and chunk support for lam-alif.

## Stage 1: First Decoding Wins

| Lesson | Anchor | New units         | Rule or pattern                                                     | Review units | Drill focus                                                                   |
| ------ | ------ | ----------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1      | `باب`  | ب, ا              | Arabic reads right to left and letters may join to neighbors.       | None         | Follow reading direction and compare isolated versus joined ba.               |
| 2      | `لا`   | ل, lam-alif chunk | Lam plus alif often appears as a compact visual chunk.              | ا            | Read a survival word and spot lam-alif in larger words.                       |
| 3      | `ماء`  | م, ء              | Hamza can mark a glottal stop and may be a separate teachable mark. | ا            | Decode a high-utility word while keeping spelling and pronunciation distinct. |
| 4      | `شاي`  | ش, ي              | Ya can mark a y sound or a long i-like vowel depending on context.  | ا            | Read a cafe word and identify final-form ya.                                  |

## Stage 2: Core Menus And Public Words

| Lesson | Anchor  | New units | Rule or pattern                                                     | Review units | Drill focus                                                    |
| ------ | ------- | --------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------ | -------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 5      | `سوق`   | س, و, ق   | Waw can work as a consonant or long-vowel letter.                   | ا            | Decode market words and distinguish nonjoining waw.            |
| 6      | `قهوة`  | ه, ة      | Ta marbuta is a common final ending with context-sensitive reading. | ق, و         | Read a cafe anchor and sort final heh versus ta marbuta.       |
| 7      | `تاكسي` | ت, ك      | Familiar loanwords can still teach Arabic joining and dot patterns. | ا, س, ي      | Build a travel word from known chunks.                         |
| 8      | `فندق`  | ف, ن, د   | Dotted families require visual contrast practice.                   | ق            | Read a hotel sign word and compare ba ta noon fa qaf.          |
| 9      | `حلال`  | ح         | Some common signs require sounds not found in English.              | ل, ا         | Read a culturally common label with reviewer-approved framing. |
| 10     | `حمام`  | ح review  | Regional signage can vary and needs validation before shipping.     | م, ا         | Decode a restroom-adjacent word and mark regional uncertainty. |

## Stage 3: Sign Pairs And Unvowelled Reading

| Lesson | Anchor | New units         | Rule or pattern                                                | Review units | Drill focus                                              |
| ------ | ------ | ----------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------ | -------------------------------------------------------- |
| 11     | `خروج` | خ, ج              | Entry and exit words can be learned as a visible sign pair.    | ر, و         | Read exit signage and practice kha versus ha families.   |
| 12     | `دخول` | د                 | Related words reuse shapes even when the first letter changes. | خ, و, ل      | Contrast entrance and exit signs.                        |
| 13     | `سعر`  | ع, ر              | Everyday Arabic often omits short vowels.                      | س            | Infer a price word from consonants and known context.    |
| 14     | `خبز`  | ز                 | Short food words can be read after a few high-load consonants. | خ, ب         | Decode a bread label and review dot placement.           |
| 15     | `حليب` | long-vowel review | Long vowels are usually represented by letters.                | ح, ل, ي, ب   | Read a menu word by chunking consonants and long vowels. |

## Stage 4: Facilities And Longer Chunks

| Lesson | Anchor  | New units          | Rule or pattern                                                         | Review units  | Drill focus                                                          |
| ------ | ------- | ------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 16     | `شارع`  | ش review, ع review | Street words reinforce real environmental print.                        | ا, ر          | Read a street-sign word with ayn.                                    |
| 17     | `مطعم`  | ط                  | Essential facility words can wait until learners can carry higher load. | م, ع          | Decode restaurant signage and introduce emphatic consonant contrast. |
| 18     | `مطار`  | ط review           | Travel words reuse emphatic ta in a shorter anchor.                     | م, ا, ر       | Read airport signage after restaurant work.                          |

## Coverage Notes

- Target-domain samples: menus, cafe boards, street and market signs, taxi and
  hotel labels, entrance and exit signs, storefront open/closed signs, restroom
  labels, and common food packaging.
- Known-grapheme or known-unit coverage goal: by Lesson 8, cover core joining
  behavior, alif, lam, ba, meem, ya, waw, seen, qaf, ha, ta marbuta, and key
  dotted contrasts; by Lesson 15, cover hamza, kha, ha, ayn, dal, zay, and long
  vowel chunking.
- Known-word or known-anchor coverage goal: early learner should recognize at
  least four survival words by Lesson 4 and a compact menu/sign set by Lesson 12.
- Deferred high-load material: full root-and-pattern morphology, dialectal
  spelling variation, Quranic or liturgical registers, handwriting, calligraphy,
  full harakat use, sun-letter assimilation, and exhaustive hamza-seat rules.
