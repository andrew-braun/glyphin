# Mongolian Reading Lesson Sequence

This staged outline targets traditional Mongolian-script reading. It is not
final learner-facing copy and it requires native-speaker and font-shaping review.

## Course Shape

- First-session reading win: read `ᠤᠰᠤ`, `ᠴᠠᠢ`, and `ᠨᠣᠮ` in a vertical-text
  friendly surface.
- Approximate lesson count: 12 core lessons plus vertical-layout and variation
  review.
- Structural family: top-to-bottom script with contextual letter forms, vowel
  harmony awareness, free variation selectors, and vertical punctuation.
- Required app support beyond the current Thai runtime: language-agnostic text
  fields, `writing-mode: vertical-lr` or equivalent layout, font shaping QA,
  variation sequence preservation, and vertical drill controls.

## Optional Review

- Add vertical punctuation and phrase-level reading only after word layout works.
- Add variation-selector lessons only where reviewers say the difference is
  necessary for beginner decoding.
- Add Cyrillic-to-Mongolian transfer notes only after the script-first course
  shape is stable.

## Coverage Notes

- Target-domain samples: food words, tea and water labels, road and market
  signs, cultural-site labels, classroom primer vocabulary, and identity labels.
- Known-unit coverage goal: by Lesson 4, learners can read short vertical words
  with core vowels and common consonants.
- Known-word coverage goal: by Lesson 8, learners can recognize water, tea,
  book, road, home, milk, meat, and bread.
- Deferred high-load material: full spelling rules, all positional alternations,
  historical variants, dictionary-level morphology, vertical punctuation, and
  copied corpus examples.
