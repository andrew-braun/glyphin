# Odia Reading Lesson Sequence

This staged outline teaches modern printed Odia as an abugida. It starts with
practical food and transit words before adding conjuncts, nukta-bearing letters,
and public-facility vocabulary.

## Course Shape

- First-session reading win: read `ପାଣି`, `ଚା`, and `ବସ` from menu and transit
  contexts.
- Approximate lesson count: 15 core lessons plus late public-facility review.
- Structural family: Odia abugida with inherent vowel, dependent vowel signs,
  anusvara, virama, rounded glyph shapes, common conjuncts, and nukta.
- Required app support beyond the current Thai runtime: language-agnostic text
  fields, akshara segmentation, matra-position metadata, conjunct metadata,
  nukta preservation, and font checks for rounded Odia glyphs and marks.

## Stage 1: First Decoding Wins

| Lesson | Anchor | New units      | Rule or pattern                                                    | Review units | Drill focus                             |
| ------ | ------ | -------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------ | ------------ | --------------------------------------- |
| 1      | `ପାଣି` | ପ, ଆ/ା, ଣ, ଇ/ି | Consonant bases carry an inherent vowel unless a matra changes it. | none         | Build aksharas from base plus matra.    |
| 2      | `ଚା`   | ଚ              | The aa sign attaches to the consonant base.                        | ଆ/ା          | Read a compact cafe word.               |
| 3      | `ବସ`   | ବ, ସ           | Bare consonants can carry the inherent vowel.                      | ଆ/ା          | Decode a transit word with known bases. |
| 4      | `ନାମ`  | ନ, ମ           | Long vowels and final bases make compact labels.                   | ଆ/ା          | Identify known bases in short words.    |

## Coverage Notes

- Target-domain samples: menus, tea stalls, bus signs, rail labels, markets,
  shopfronts, school labels, hospitals or clinics, open/closed signs, and Odisha
  place names.
- Known-unit coverage goal: by Lesson 9, learners handle common bases and
  high-payoff matras; by Lesson 15, they have seen virama clusters and a
  nukta-bearing place-name letter.
- Deferred high-load material: full conjunct inventory, complete aspirated and
  retroflex contrast tables, Odia digits, handwriting, vowel reduction, and
  long public-facility words such as `ଶୌଚାଳୟ`.
