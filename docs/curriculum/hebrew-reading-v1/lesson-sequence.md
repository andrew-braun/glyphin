# Hebrew Reading Lesson Sequence

This staged outline is a review-ready starting point, not final learner-facing
lesson copy. It teaches practical Modern Hebrew reading before full grammar,
production spelling, or pointed-text study.

## Course Shape

- First-session reading win: read `לא`, `מים`, `קפה`, and `שלום` as real words.
- Approximate lesson count: 16 core lessons plus optional facility and
  storefront consolidation.
- Structural family: right-to-left abjad with final letter forms, vowel letters
  in unpointed text, optional niqqud, no case, and several dagesh-sensitive
  sound pairs.
- Required app support beyond the current Thai runtime: RTL rendering, bidi
  isolation for mixed-script UI, language-agnostic text fields, final-form
  metadata, optional niqqud overlays, and course-aware script styling.

## Stage 1: First Decoding Wins

| Lesson | Anchor | New units | Rule or pattern                                                        | Review units | Drill focus                                           |
| ------ | ------ | --------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------ | ----------------------------------------------------- |
| 1      | `לא`   | ל, א      | Hebrew reads right to left and alef may be a silent carrier.           | None         | Follow direction and read a compact survival word.    |
| 2      | `מים`  | מ/ם, י    | Some Hebrew letters have special final forms at word end.              | None         | Recognize mem and final mem in one high-utility word. |
| 3      | `קפה`  | ק, פ/ף, ה | Final he often marks an a/e-like ending in unpointed words.            | א            | Read a familiar cafe word and compare pe forms.       |
| 4      | `שלום` | ש, ו      | Vav can represent a consonant or a vowel-like sound in unpointed text. | ל, מ/ם       | Read a culturally familiar word and review final mem. |

## Coverage Notes

- Target-domain samples: cafe boards, restaurant signs, market labels, street and
  transit signs, entrance and exit labels, restroom signs, hotel signs, price
  labels, and storefront open/closed signs.
- Known-grapheme or known-unit coverage goal: by Lesson 8, cover alef, lamed,
  mem/final mem, yod, qof, pe, he, shin, vav, kaf/final kaf, nun/final nun, het,
  and bet; by Lesson 16, cover samekh, ayin, dalet, resh, tsadi/final tsadi, tav,
  and tet.
- Known-word or known-anchor coverage goal: early learner should recognize at
  least four practical words by Lesson 4 and a compact menu/sign set by Lesson 13.
- Deferred high-load material: full niqqud, cantillation marks, biblical or
  liturgical registers, handwriting, production spelling, root-and-pattern
  morphology, stress rules, and detailed dagesh behavior.
