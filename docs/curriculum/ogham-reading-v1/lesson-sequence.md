# Ogham Reading Lesson Sequence

This staged outline is a review-ready starting point, not final learner-facing
lesson copy. It uses horizontal Unicode for authoring but treats stone-edge
orientation as explicit metadata.

## Course Shape

- First-session reading win: decode `ᚋᚐᚊᚊᚔ` and understand it as a formulaic
  inscription chunk rather than ordinary modern text.
- Approximate lesson count: 11 core lessons plus optional stone-orientation
  review.
- Structural family: alphabetic inscription script with a stemline, aicme groups,
  formulaic Old Irish fragments, and mixed digital versus stone orientation.
- Required app support beyond the current Thai runtime: orientation metadata,
  stemline-aware display checks, transliteration fields, start and end mark
  handling, and formula-fragment source notes.

## Coverage Notes

- Target-domain samples: museum stone labels, Ogham in 3D style records,
  heritage-site signage, inscription drawings, and beginner epigraphy guides.
- Known-grapheme or known-unit coverage goal: by Lesson 5, learners know the
  letters needed for the most common formula chunks plus boundary marks.
- Known-word or known-anchor coverage goal: learners can identify MAQI, MUCOI,
  and AVI style fragments in controlled examples.
- Deferred high-load material: full inscription editions, exact Old Irish
  morphology, vertical stone rendering, stemline animation, later forfeda,
  damaged inscriptions, and unreviewed personal names.
