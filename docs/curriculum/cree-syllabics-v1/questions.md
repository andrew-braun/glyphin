# Cree Syllabics Reading Questions

Use this file for unresolved decisions that research cannot safely settle during
the `cree-syllabics-v1` bootstrap.

## Architecture

- How should the runtime represent syllabic orientation series and finals without
  forcing alphabet-style categories?
- Should dialect and orthography tags live on course versions, lessons, anchors,
  graphemes, or all of those records?
- How should w-dot and long-vowel behavior be stored so matching and display stay
  stable?

## Product And Pedagogy

- Which Cree dialect and spelling conventions define v1?
- Should first lessons follow greeting-first pedagogy or a chart-family order
  preferred by Cree educators?
- Which target domains are appropriate for a learner-facing product without
  overstepping community context?

## Sources, Licensing, And Attribution

- Confirm terms for Cree Dictionary Online and learner sites before using their
  examples beyond discovery.
- Keep external teaching material out of shipped content unless permissions and
  attribution are resolved.
- Prefer app-authored examples reviewed by Cree language experts.

## Reviewers And Validation

- Assign Cree language reviewers for dialect, spelling, pronunciation, and
  cultural context.
- Verify fonts render full syllabic signs, finals, and dots clearly at drill
  sizes.
- Confirm anchor candidates against real community and classroom usage.

## App Expansion Recommendations

- Add syllabary-series metadata with orientation and final roles.
- Add per-course dialect and orthography notes surfaced to reviewers.
- Add font and accessibility checks for small final signs.
