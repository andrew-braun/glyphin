# Cree Syllabics Reading Curriculum Notes

This durable course note records the v1 boundary and bootstrap decisions for a
Cree syllabics reading course.

## Course Boundary

- Language/script: Cree in Canadian Aboriginal Syllabics, `cr-Cans`, ISO 15924
  `Cans`.
- Target learner: community learner, heritage learner, Canadian cultural-site
  visitor, or script enthusiast who wants a careful first pass at Cree syllabics.
- Target domains: community language signage, learning materials, dictionary
  headwords, cultural-site labels, and short public labels.
- In scope for v1: a Plains Cree-oriented subset, orientation-based vowel series,
  finals, a few reviewed short anchors, and app-authored examples.
- Out of scope for v1: pan-Cans coverage, Inuktitut or Ojibwe syllabics,
  dialect-complete Cree coverage, full grammar, handwriting, and copied examples.

## Sequencing Rationale

- Frequency sources: Unicode and CLDR metadata for inventory, Cree dictionary and
  learning resources for discovery, and app-authored examples for shipped
  content.
- First-session decoding target: `ᑖᓂᓯ` because it is memorable and exposes the
  orientation logic that makes syllabics teachable.
- Stage 1 goal: teach signs as syllables and introduce the orientation system.
- Stage 2 goal: teach finals through compact words and reviewer-approved
  natural-world anchors.
- Stage 3 goal: add community labels only after dialect and source approval.

## Script Notes

- Cans is a unified script used by multiple languages. A Cree course needs a
  scoped inventory and should not imply universal syllabics coverage.
- Finals are crucial for reading but are visually and pedagogically different
  from full syllabic signs.
- Local orthography decisions affect long vowels, w-dots, finals, and selected
  signs.

## Validation Notes

- Segmentation review: verify syllabic signs, finals, long-vowel marks, and
  dialect conventions.
- Pronunciation review: confirm all anchors with Cree language experts and label
  dialect assumptions.
- Cultural review: make sure identity, land, animal, and public-label examples
  are appropriate for learner display.
- License review: keep external dictionaries and teaching materials discovery or
  scoring only until terms are approved.
