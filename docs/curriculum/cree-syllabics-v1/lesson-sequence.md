# Cree Syllabics Reading Lesson Sequence

This staged outline is a review-ready starting point, not final learner-facing
lesson copy. It assumes a Plains Cree-oriented v1 path and must be reviewed by
Cree language experts before implementation.

## Course Shape

- First-session reading win: read `ᑖᓂᓯ` and identify orientation-based syllabic
  signs.
- Approximate lesson count: 12 core lessons plus dialect-specific extensions.
- Structural family: Canadian Aboriginal syllabics with orientation-based vowels,
  finals, and language-specific inventories.
- Required app support beyond the current Thai runtime: syllabic series metadata,
  finals handling, dialect/orthography tags, font coverage, and community review
  status.

## Stage 1: First Decoding Wins

| Lesson | Anchor       | New units             | Rule or pattern                                                          | Review units | Drill focus                                              |
| ------ | ------------ | --------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ------------ | -------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1      | `ᑖᓂᓯ`        | ᑖ, ᓂ, ᓯ               | Syllabics signs represent syllables and orientation carries vowel value. | None         | Match signs to syllables in a greeting.                  |
| 2      | `ᓂᐱᐩ`        | ᐱ, final-y convention | Finals are small signs that close syllables.                             | ᓂ            | Read a compact water candidate after reviewer approval.  |
| 3      | Vowel series | ᐊ, ᐃ, ᐅ, ᐁ            | Rotation and orientation are core reading cues.                          | ᑖ            | Sort signs by vowel orientation.                         |
| 4      | `ᓀᐦᐃᔭᐤ`      | ᓀ, ᐦ, ᔭ, ᐤ            | Identity words require dialect and cultural review.                      | ᓂ, ᓯ         | Chunk a longer identity anchor without overgeneralizing. |

## Stage 2: Finals And Useful Words

| Lesson | Anchor  | New units  | Rule or pattern                                               | Review units | Drill focus                                       |
| ------ | ------- | ---------- | ------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------ | ------------------------------------------------- |
| 5      | `ᐊᒥᐢᐠ`  | ᐊ, ᒥ, ᐢ, ᐠ | Finals must be read as consonants rather than full syllables. | ᐱ            | Identify final s and final k in short words.      |
| 6      | `ᐃᐢᑯᑌᐤ` | ᐃ, ᑯ, ᑌ    | Long words can be decoded by chunking syllabics plus finals.  | ᐢ, ᐤ         | Build a word from known signs and one new series. |
| 7      | `ᒥᐢᑕᐦᐃ` | ᑕ, ᐦ       | Final h needs explicit visual and pronunciation treatment.    | ᒥ, ᐢ         | Contrast final signs in recognition drills.       |
| 8      | `ᐊᐢᑭᐩ`  | ᑭ          | Land and place words need cultural context.                   | ᐊ, ᐢ, ᐠ      | Read a domain-rich candidate after review.        |

## Stage 3: Domain Text And Orthography Choices

| Lesson | Anchor                  | New units                | Rule or pattern                                                          | Review units   | Drill focus                                         |
| ------ | ----------------------- | ------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------ | -------------- | --------------------------------------------------- |
| 9      | Community label set     | reviewer-selected labels | Local usage determines which words belong in public-label lessons.       | Stage 1 units  | Spot known syllabics in short labels.               |

## Coverage Notes

- Target-domain samples: community signage, language classes, cultural labels,
  dictionary headwords, and short public labels.
- Known-unit coverage goal: establish a minimal vowel orientation model and the
  most common finals before adding longer words.
- Known-anchor coverage goal: greeting, water candidate, identity word, and one
  final-heavy anchor after reviewer approval.
- Deferred high-load material: full Cans script coverage, Inuktitut and Ojibwe
  inventories, local spelling variation, full grammar, audio production, and
  unreviewed teaching examples.
