# Georgian Reading Lesson Sequence

This staged outline targets modern Georgian Mkhedruli reading for practical
menus, signs, labels, transit text, and cultural-site words.

## Course Shape

- First-session reading win: read `ჩაი`, `პური`, and `მენიუ` as useful cafe and
  menu words.
- Approximate lesson count: 14 core lessons plus optional food and greeting
  review.
- Structural family: alphabetic Mkhedruli script with no everyday case pairs and
  a high consonant-contrast load.
- Required app support beyond the current Thai runtime: language-agnostic text
  fields, Georgian font sizing checks, contrast-family metadata for ejective and
  aspirated consonants, and course-aware progress storage.

| ------ | ------- | --------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- | ------------ | --------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1      | `ჩაი`   | ჩ, ა, ი   | Georgian letters are read left to right with stable values. | none         | Decode a short cafe word.                                 |
| 2      | `პური`  | პ, უ, რ   | პ is an ejective p-like sound and needs careful contrast.   | ი            | Read a food word and identify vowel sequence.             |
| 3      | `მენიუ` | მ, ე, ნ   | Loanwords can provide low-load menu wins.                   | ი, უ         | Read menu labels and chunk familiar vowels.               |
| 4      | `ყავა`  | ყ, ვ      | ყ is high-load but unlocks a core cafe word.                | ა            | Recognize coffee while flagging pronunciation for review. |

## Optional Review

- Add `ხაჭაპური`, `აფთიაქი`, `სასტუმრო`, `ღია`, `დაკეტილია`, and
  `გამარჯობა` after core signs are stable.
- Add Mtavruli sign examples only if real target-domain samples justify them.
- Add audio-backed consonant contrast drills before production-style tasks.

## Coverage Notes

- Target-domain samples: cafe boards, restaurant signs, metro labels, bus stop
  signs, street labels, market signs, restroom labels, pharmacy signs, museum
  labels, open and closed storefronts, and hotel signs.
- Known-unit coverage goal: by Lesson 8, learners should read short cafe words,
  menu labels, metro, restaurant, water, and stop.
- Known-word coverage goal: by Lesson 14, learners should recognize core food,
  transit, facility, price, street, museum, entrance, and exit anchors.
- Deferred high-load material: full consonant contrast theory, production
  pronunciation, historical scripts, handwriting, Mtavruli as a general case
  system, and grammar.
