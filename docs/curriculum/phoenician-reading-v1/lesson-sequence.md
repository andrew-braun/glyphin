# Phoenician Reading Lesson Sequence

This staged outline is a review-ready starting point, not final learner-facing
lesson copy. It uses consonant skeletons and transliteration labels rather than
trying to reconstruct fluent pronunciation.

## Course Shape

- First-session reading win: read `𐤌𐤋𐤊`, `𐤁𐤍`, and `𐤔𐤌` as compact inscription
  words with transliteration.
- Approximate lesson count: 12 core lessons plus optional alphabet-chart review.
- Structural family: right-to-left consonantal abjad with absent vowels,
  transliteration conventions, and specialist-reviewed inscription formulae.
- Required app support beyond the current Thai runtime: RTL script display,
  transliteration fields, consonant-skeleton modeling, vowel-omission notes,
  and Semitic consonant pronunciation caveats.

## Coverage Notes

- Target-domain samples: museum labels, inscription drawings, alphabet charts,
  scholarly transliterations, and curated beginner epigraphy examples.
- Known-grapheme or known-unit coverage goal: by Lesson 7, learners know the
  most useful letters for short inscription words and genealogical formulae.
- Known-word or known-anchor coverage goal: learners can identify several
  common consonant skeletons and read them with cautious glosses.
- Deferred high-load material: full inscription grammar, matres lectionis,
  regional letter variants, exact vocalization, Punic developments, lapidary
  paleography, and copied inscription passages.
