# Tamil Reading Lesson Sequence

This staged outline is a review-ready starting point, not final learner-facing
lesson copy. It targets practical Tamil reading before full script-chart
completion.

## Course Shape

- First-session reading win: read `கடை`, `காபி`, and `பால்` on shop or menu
  contexts.
- Approximate lesson count: 15 core lessons plus late review for dense facility
  words and loan letters.
- Structural family: South Indic abugida with consonant bases, inherent vowel,
  dependent vowel signs, pulli, contextual stop values, and limited conjunct
  load compared with other Indic scripts.
- Required app support beyond the current Thai runtime: language-agnostic text
  fields, teachable units inside aksharas, combining-mark display metadata,
  pulli handling, script-aware fonts, and per-course progress state.

## Stage 1: First Decoding Wins

| Lesson | Anchor | New units    | Rule or pattern                                                    | Review units | Drill focus                                                    |
| ------ | ------ | ------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------ | ------------ | -------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1      | `கடை`  | க, ட, ஐ/ை    | A consonant base carries an inherent `a` unless a sign changes it. | None         | Build a shop word from two consonant bases and one vowel sign. |
| 2      | `காபி` | ஆ/ா, ப, இ/ி  | Dependent vowel signs attach to consonant bases.                   | க            | Read a cafe loanword by chunking visible signs.                |
| 3      | `பால்` | ல, pulli `்` | Pulli suppresses the inherent vowel.                               | ப, ஆ/ா       | Contrast `ல` and `ல்` as open and closed consonant units.      |
| 4      | `விலை` | வ, ல review  | Tamil signs can appear around a base but belong to that syllable.  | இ/ி, ஐ/ை     | Read a shopping word with repeated known signs.                |

## Stage 2: Core Menus And Transit

| Lesson | Anchor   | New units      | Rule or pattern                                                 | Review units | Drill focus                                                    |
| ------ | -------- | -------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------ | -------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 5      | `அரிசி`  | அ, ர, ச        | Independent vowels start syllables without a consonant base.    | இ/ி          | Decode a food word with repeated i signs.                      |
| 6      | `தேநீர்` | த, ந, ஏ/ே, ஈ/ீ | Pre-base vowel signs still belong to the following consonant.   | ர            | Read cafe words with long vowels and visual reordering.        |
| 7      | `ரயில்`  | ய              | Pulli can close a short high-utility transit word.              | ர, இ/ி, ல்   | Identify dead consonants at word end.                          |
| 8      | `உணவு`   | உ/ு, ண         | Tamil has multiple n-like consonants that need contrast drills. | வ            | Recognize a menu category while flagging retroflex nasal load. |

## Stage 3: Facilities And Script-Specific Contrasts

| Lesson | Anchor      | New units   | Rule or pattern                                                  | Review units | Drill focus                                                   |
| ------ | ----------- | ----------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 9      | `பஸ்`       | ஸ           | Some loanwords use Grantha letters and pulli-heavy endings.      | ப, pulli     | Decide whether loan letters belong in v1 core or late review. |
| 10     | `மருந்து`   | ம, து chunk | Tamil stop values require reviewer-approved pronunciation notes. | ர, ந, உ/ு    | Read a medicine word without overteaching phonology.          |
| 11     | `மருந்தகம்` | கம் chunk   | Longer facility words can be taught by known chunks.             | மருந்து, க   | Chunk pharmacy signage.                                       |
| 12     | `நுழைவு`    | ழ           | ழ is high cultural value but high pronunciation load.            | ந, வ, ஐ/ை    | Separate visual recognition from perfect pronunciation.       |

## Stage 4: High-Load Survival Words

| Lesson | Anchor       | New units     | Rule or pattern                                                                | Review units  | Drill focus                                          |
| ------ | ------------ | ------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ------------- | ---------------------------------------------------- |
| 13     | `வெளியே`     | எ/ெ, ள        | Pre-base e and retroflex lateral need clear font rendering.                    | வ, ய          | Read exit/outside signs.                             |
| 14     | `தண்ணீர்`    | geminate ண்ண  | Conjunct-like doubled consonants should be introduced through one useful word. | த, ந, ஈ/ீ, ர் | Decode a survival word with high new load.           |
| 15     | `கழிப்பிடம்` | ழ review, டம் | Facility terms may be long and should use staged chunking.                     | க, ப, இ/ி, ட  | Read toilet signage after enough units are familiar. |

## Coverage Notes

- Target-domain samples: shop signs, cafe menus, packaged food labels, bus and
  rail labels, pharmacy signs, restroom signs, and simple entrance or exit text.
- Known-grapheme or known-unit coverage goal: by Lesson 8, cover the highest
  payoff vowel signs and common consonants needed for menu and transit words; by
  Lesson 12, add pulli fluency, loan-letter awareness, and Tamil-specific liquid
  contrasts.
- Known-word or known-anchor coverage goal: learner recognizes at least six
  useful words by Lesson 8 and a compact facility set by Lesson 15.
- Deferred high-load material: full vowel inventory, rare Grantha letters,
  aytham, numerals, poetic or classical forms, handwriting, production spelling,
  detailed stop allophony, and exhaustive consonant contrasts.
