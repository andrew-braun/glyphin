# Linear B Reading Lesson Sequence

This staged outline is a review-ready starting point, not final learner-facing
lesson copy. It targets tablet literacy and transliteration rather than fluent
Ancient Greek pronunciation.

## Course Shape

- First-session reading win: decode `𐀏𐀒` as `ka-ko` and understand it as a
  tablet-domain word for bronze after review.
- Approximate lesson count: 11 core lessons plus optional ideogram and damaged
  tablet expansion.
- Structural family: syllabary with administrative ideograms, tablet notation,
  transliteration syllables, and Mycenaean Greek interpretation.
- Required app support beyond the current Thai runtime: syllabogram units,
  transliteration display, ideogram metadata, tablet context fields, damaged-text
  notes, and nonmodern pronunciation labels.

## Coverage Notes

- Target-domain samples: museum tablet labels, syllabary charts, administrative
  word lists, tablet transliterations, and beginner Aegean scripts guides.
- Known-grapheme or known-unit coverage goal: by Lesson 7, learners know a small
  high-payoff set of syllabograms used in common tablet words and names.
- Known-word or known-anchor coverage goal: learners can decode bronze, king,
  Knossos, Potnia, swords, and tripod style anchors with review caveats.
- Deferred high-load material: full syllabary, all ideograms, numerals, damaged
  tablets, full Mycenaean grammar, dialect comparison, and exact reconstructed
  pronunciation.
