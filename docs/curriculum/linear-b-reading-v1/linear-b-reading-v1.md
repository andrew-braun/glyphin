# Linear B Reading Curriculum Notes

This durable course note records the v1 boundary and bootstrap decisions for a
Linear B reading course.

## Course Boundary

- Language/script: Mycenaean Greek in Linear B, `gmy-Linb`, ISO 15924 `Linb`.
- Target learner: museum visitor, Aegean scripts beginner, or historical-language
  learner who wants to recognize selected syllabograms and tablet words.
- Target domains: clay tablet labels, syllabary charts, administrative terms,
  museum captions, and scholarly transliterations.
- In scope for v1: selected syllabograms, a few high-payoff tablet words,
  transliteration, basic syllabary constraints, and ideogram planning notes.
- Out of scope for v1: full Mycenaean grammar, full sign list, all ideograms,
  numerals, damaged-text restoration, dialect reconstruction, and fluent
  pronunciation.

## Sequencing Rationale

- Frequency sources: Unicode metadata for syllabogram and ideogram inventory,
  DĀMOS for scoring-only tablet frequency intuition, Aegean scripts references
  for discovery-only sign conventions, Wiktionary for gloss discovery, Noto font
  checks for rendering, and app-authored examples for shipped content.
- First-session decoding target: `𐀏𐀒`, because `ka-ko` is compact and tied to a
  concrete tablet-domain gloss after review.
- Stage 1 goal: establish syllabograms and transliteration through short words.
- Stage 2 goal: add administrative objects, place names, and titles while
  teaching chunking.
- Stage 3 goal: introduce harder conventions such as omitted consonants,
  labiovelars, and fragment confidence.

## Script Notes

- Linear B signs mostly represent open syllables. Greek consonant clusters and
  final consonants can be absent or represented indirectly.
- The script has separate ideograms and numerals that matter in tablets but are
  too much for the first syllabogram pass.
- Transliteration such as `ka-ko` is a scholarly convention and should stay
  separate from both the script signs and any interpreted Greek word.
- Pronunciation should be labeled as Mycenaean or classroom convention rather
  than modern Greek.

## Validation Notes

- Segmentation review: verify each syllabogram maps to one pedagogical unit and
  one transliteration syllable.
- Pronunciation review: use a Mycenaean Greek or Aegean scripts specialist for
  transliteration, glosses, and interpretation notes.
- Cultural review: administrative and sacred titles need context before becoming
  learner-facing vocabulary.
- License review: keep tablet database records, editions, and transliterations
  out of shipped content until reuse terms are approved.
