# Morse Signaling Reading Lesson Sequence

This staged outline targets International Morse recognition. Dot-dash text is a
review notation; the real learning target is timed audio, light, or haptic
signals.

## Course Shape

- First-session reading win: recognize `.` as E, `-` as T, and `... --- ...` as
  SOS in symbolic notation while naming timing gaps.
- Approximate lesson count: 14 core lessons plus optional prosign and number
  review.
- Structural family: time-based signaling system represented by symbols, gaps,
  letters, words, and prosigns.
- Required app support beyond the current Thai runtime: notation-course modeling,
  timed audio playback, light or haptic rendering, speed and Farnsworth metadata,
  gap-aware scoring, prosign roles, and accessibility controls.

## Stage 1: Signal Elements And First Letters

| Lesson | Anchor | New units | Rule or pattern                          | Review units | Drill focus                                       |
| ------ | ------ | --------- | ---------------------------------------- | ------------ | ------------------------------------------------- |
| 1      | `.`    | dit       | A dit is the short signal unit.          | none         | Distinguish signal from notation.                 |
| 2      | `-`    | dah       | A dah is longer than a dit.              | dit          | Contrast short and long signals.                  |
| 3      | `..`   | I         | Repetition creates a letter pattern.     | E            | Recognize repeated dits and intra-character gaps. |
| 4      | `.-`   | A         | Order matters inside a character.        | E, T         | Contrast dit-dah and dah-dit.                     |
| 5      | `-.`   | N         | Mirror patterns need careful gap timing. | A            | Sort A and N by order.                            |

## Stage 2: High-Value Words And Distress Context

| Lesson | Anchor        | New units    | Rule or pattern                                         | Review units | Drill focus                             |
| ------ | ------------- | ------------ | ------------------------------------------------------- | ------------ | --------------------------------------- |
| 6      | `...`         | S            | Three dits form a common emergency component.           | E, I         | Recognize repeated short signals.       |
| 7      | `---`         | O            | Three dahs contrast with three dits.                    | T            | Contrast S and O.                       |
| 8      | `... --- ...` | SOS sequence | Character and word gaps are semantic.                   | S, O         | Recognize SOS with spacing and context. |
| 9      | `-. ---`      | NO           | Word reading joins letter patterns with character gaps. | N, O         | Decode a short word.                    |
| 10     | `--- -.-`     | OK           | A familiar word can justify K.                          | O            | Read a short acknowledgement.           |

## Stage 3: Practical Words And Procedure

| Lesson | Anchor             | New units | Rule or pattern                                     | Review units  | Drill focus                              |
| ------ | ------------------ | --------- | --------------------------------------------------- | ------------- | ---------------------------------------- |
| 11     | `.... . .-.. .--.` | HELP      | Emergency words need timing and sensitivity review. | E, A          | Decode a high-value word after H L P.    |
| 12     | `... - --- .--.`   | STOP      | Public words can be read as letter sequences.       | S, T, O, P    | Read stop and compare with SOS.          |
| 13     | `-.-- . ...`       | YES       | Later letters can be added by word payoff.          | E, S          | Contrast yes and no.                     |
| 14     | `- .-. .- .. -.`   | TRAIN     | Practical words can review common letters.          | T, R, A, I, N | Decode a transit word and gap structure. |

## Optional Review

- Add `WATER`, `FOOD`, `EXIT`, `OPEN`, `BUS`, `CALL`, and `CQ` after core
  timing and gaps are stable.
- Add numerals only after letter timing is reliable.
- Add prosigns such as AR only after the model supports no-letter-gap grouped
  symbols.

## Coverage Notes

- Target-domain samples: emergency recognition, radio practice, scout learning,
  maritime and aviation heritage examples, and accessible audio or haptic drills.
- Known-unit coverage goal: by Lesson 8, learners understand dit, dah,
  intra-character gap, character gap, S, O, and SOS as a spaced sequence.
- Known-word coverage goal: by Lesson 14, learners recognize E, T, I, A, N, S,
  O, K, SOS, NO, OK, HELP, STOP, YES, and TRAIN.
- Deferred high-load material: WPM and Farnsworth tuning, full punctuation,
  numerals, prosigns, radio operating procedure, sending skill, and formal
  emergency training.
