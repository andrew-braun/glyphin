# Sitelen Pona Reading Lesson Sequence

This staged outline is a review-ready starting point, not final learner-facing
lesson copy. It uses glyph IDs as placeholders until a font and encoding policy
is approved.

## Course Shape

- First-session reading win: identify the `toki` and `pona` glyph IDs in `toki
pona`.
- Approximate lesson count: 10 core lessons plus glyph-variant extensions.
- Structural family: compact logographic constructed script with font-specific
  glyphs, possible PUA encodings, compounds, and community variants.
- Required app support beyond the current Thai runtime: non-Unicode glyph IDs,
  font licensing, glyph accessibility labels, variant metadata, compound
  segmentation, and community review status.

| ------ | ---------------- | ---------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------ | ------------------------------------------------- |

| 2      | `mi moku`        | mi, moku   | Simple sentences can be read as glyph sequences.                       | pona         | Read a compact app-authored food sentence.        |

## Coverage Notes

- Target-domain samples: community learning materials, short app-authored
  sentences, glyph identity drills, constructed-script culture, and font tests.
- Known-unit coverage goal: teach a compact set of high-frequency glyph IDs
  before compounds and grammar-heavy sentences.
- Known-anchor coverage goal: approve `toki pona`, `mi moku`, `telo`, and `moku`
  as the first mini-set after glyph policy is settled.
- Deferred high-load material: PUA publication, official Unicode assumptions,
  glyph variant comparison, cartouches, copied community examples, and sitelen
  sitelen.
