# Sinhala Reading Lesson Sequence

This staged outline is a review-ready starting point, not final learner-facing
lesson copy. It targets practical Sinhala reading before full akshara or grammar
mastery.

## Course Shape

- First-session reading win: read `තේ`, `බත්`, and `වතුර` in tea, food, and
  survival contexts.
- Approximate lesson count: 16 core lessons plus late public-facility review.
- Structural family: Sinhala abugida with consonant bases, inherent vowel,
  dependent vowel signs, al-lakuna, anusvara, conjunct or ligature behavior, and
  visible word spacing.
- Required app support beyond the current Thai runtime: language-agnostic text
  fields, akshara segmentation, vowel-position metadata, al-lakuna metadata,
  no-split display spans for conjuncts, Sinhala font QA, and course-aware
  progress state.

## Coverage Notes

- Target-domain samples: tea shops, menus, shop signs, price labels, bus and
  train signs, road signs, school and civic labels, hotel signs, hospital signs,
  open and closed signs, entrance or exit signs, and toilets.
- Known-grapheme or known-unit coverage goal: by Lesson 4, cover first vowel
  signs, al-lakuna, simple aksharas, and price words; by Lesson 12, add shop,
  transit, road, hotel, hospital, and train chunks.
- Known-word or known-anchor coverage goal: learner reads at least seven useful
  tea, food, shop, price, transit, and road words by Lesson 8 and a compact
  public-sign set by Lesson 17.
- Deferred high-load material: full pure and mixed Sinhala inventories, full
  conjunct inventory, rare signs, Sinhala digits, handwriting, production
  spelling, morphology, and grammar.
