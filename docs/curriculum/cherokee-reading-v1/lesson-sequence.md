# Cherokee Reading Lesson Sequence

This staged outline is a review-ready starting point, not final learner-facing
lesson copy. It targets modern Cherokee syllabary recognition in respectful
revitalization and community-learning contexts.

## Course Shape

- First-session reading win: read `ᎣᏏᏲ`, `ᏩᏙ`, and the first signs in `ᏣᎳᎩ`.
- Approximate lesson count: 12 core lessons plus review-gated expansion.
- Structural family: left-to-right syllabary with a large encoded inventory and
  modern case support.
- Required app support beyond the current Thai runtime: syllabary inventory
  metadata, Cherokee font coverage, case-policy fields, community-review notes,
  and language-agnostic text fields.

## Coverage Notes

- Target-domain samples: community language signage, school or cultural labels,
  dictionary headwords, revitalization learning material, and short public text.
- Known-unit coverage goal: teach a compact set of high-payoff syllabograms
  before expanding into full chart order.
- Known-anchor coverage goal: establish greeting, gratitude, identity, and one
  everyday noun by Lesson 4 if reviewers approve the forms.
- Deferred high-load material: full 85-character inventory, lowercase-first
  pedagogy, dialect comparison, verb morphology, production spelling, and any
  copied third-party examples.
