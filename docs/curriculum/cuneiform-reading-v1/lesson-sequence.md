# Sumerian Cuneiform Reading Lesson Sequence

This staged outline targets sign recognition and transliteration-aware decoding
for a small Sumerian cuneiform v1. It is not final learner-facing lesson copy
and requires Assyriology review before publication.

## Course Shape

- First-session reading win: recognize `𒀀`, `𒆠`, and `𒂍` as real signs with
  reviewed sign values.
- Approximate lesson count: 12 compact lessons plus optional tablet-label review.
- Structural family: logosyllabic historic script with polyvalent signs,
  determinatives, damaged-source conventions, and transliteration dependency.
- Required app support beyond the current Thai runtime: sign-value metadata,
  transliteration display, facsimile/source notes, determinative tagging,
  cuneiform font fallback, and specialist-review gates.

## Coverage Notes

- Target-domain samples: museum labels, royal and dedicatory inscriptions,
  transliteration tables, object labels, and introductory sign lists.
- Known-unit coverage goal: by Lesson 8, learners can identify core place,
  temple, title, royal, and determinative signs in reviewed contexts.
- Known-anchor coverage goal: learners recognize at least six high-salience
  inscription signs without mistaking sign values for full language knowledge.
- Deferred high-load material: Akkadian readings, full sign lists, paleographic
  variants, damaged tablet notation, grammar, and unreviewed sign compounds.
