# Mandarin Bopomofo Reading Lesson Sequence

This v1 teaches Bopomofo as Mandarin pronunciation notation. Hanzi and pinyin
may appear as review metadata, but the learner-facing reading target is Zhuyin.

## Course Shape

- First-session reading win: read `ㄇㄚ`, contrast `ㄇㄚˇ`, and decode
  `ㄋㄧˇ ㄏㄠˇ` as a real greeting.
- Approximate lesson count: 16 lessons.
- Structural family: phonetic syllable notation with initials, medials, finals,
  explicit tone marks, unmarked first tone, and neutral-tone dot behavior.
- Required app support beyond the current Thai runtime: language-agnostic text
  fields, syllable-group segmentation, Bopomofo tone-mark placement, optional
  Hanzi and pinyin support fields, and script-specific font checks.

## Coverage Notes

- Target-domain samples: Taiwanese learner materials, dictionary annotations,
  Bopomofo ruby text, menu vocabulary, price terms, store status, entrance/exit,
  and public-facility labels.
- Known-unit goal: by Lesson 9, learners know core initials, finals, and three
  visible tone marks through food and drink words; by Lesson 16, they can read
  a compact wayfinding and facility set in Bopomofo.
- Known-word goal: early learners decode greetings, water, meal, tea, coffee,
  open/closed, entrance/exit, and male/female notation.
- Deferred high-load material: full Bopomofo extended symbols, dialectal
  notation, vertical ruby layout, detailed tone sandhi, er-hua, keyboard input,
  handwriting, and full Han reading.
