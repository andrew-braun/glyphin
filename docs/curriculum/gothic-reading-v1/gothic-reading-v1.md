# Gothic Reading Curriculum Notes

This durable course note records the v1 boundary and bootstrap decisions for a
Gothic reading course.

## Course Boundary

- Language/script: Gothic in Gothic script, `got-Goth`, ISO 15924 `Goth`.
- Target learner: historical-language beginner, manuscript learner, museum
  visitor, or Germanic-languages learner who wants to recognize Gothic words.
- Target domains: biblical manuscript transcriptions, lexical headwords, museum
  and cultural sites, alphabet tables, and transliteration workbenches.
- In scope for v1: core Gothic letters, repeated-letter chunking,
  transliteration labels, selected short words, and sacred-context awareness.
- Out of scope for v1: full Gothic grammar, exact biblical verses, manuscript
  paleography, numeric letter use, and production writing.

## Sequencing Rationale

- Frequency sources: Unicode metadata, Gothic corpus references, Wulfila Project
  for discovery, Wiktionary for discovery-only gloss checks, font checks, and
  app-authored examples for shipped content.
- First-session decoding target: `𐍃𐌿𐌽𐌿𐍃`, `𐌰𐍄𐍄𐌰`, and `𐌽𐌰𐌼𐍉`, because they
  are compact and show repeated-letter chunking.
- Stage 1 goal: build confidence with visible alphabetic decoding.
- Stage 2 goal: add sacred and manuscript-domain words with context.
- Stage 3 goal: add distinctive Gothic letters and higher-load chunks.

## Script Notes

- Gothic is alphabetic and left-to-right but uses distinctive letters that can
  resemble Greek or Latin forms.
- The corpus is strongly biblical and edition-dependent, so cultural and source
  notes are part of the course boundary.
- Pronunciation hints should be reviewed and labeled as reconstructed.
- Font support must be checked because Gothic lives in the SMP range.

## Validation Notes

- Segmentation review: verify Gothic code points, transliteration labels,
  punctuation, and edition conventions.
- Pronunciation review: confirm reconstructed values and avoid unreviewed audio.
- Cultural review: handle biblical and sacred terms respectfully.
- License review: keep corpus and edition text out of shipped content until reuse
  obligations are approved.
