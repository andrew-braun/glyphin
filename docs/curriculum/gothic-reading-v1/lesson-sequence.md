# Gothic Reading Lesson Sequence

This staged outline targets beginner Gothic alphabet recognition in biblical,
lexical, and museum contexts. It requires Gothic specialist review before
learner-facing publication.

## Course Shape

- First-session reading win: read `𐍃𐌿𐌽𐌿𐍃`, `𐌰𐍄𐍄𐌰`, and `𐌽𐌰𐌼𐍉` as real
  Gothic words.
- Approximate lesson count: 12 lessons plus optional sacred-text review.
- Structural family: alphabetic historic script with Greek-derived forms,
  biblical corpus skew, transliteration conventions, and sacred vocabulary.
- Required app support beyond the current Thai runtime: Gothic font fallback,
  transliteration fields, sacred-context metadata, source edition notes, and
  historic-language review status.

## Coverage Notes

- Target-domain samples: biblical manuscript transcriptions, lexical headwords,
  museum labels, alphabet tables, and transliteration workbenches.
- Known-unit coverage goal: by Lesson 8, learners recognize core vowels,
  repeated consonants, sacred-text anchors, and a few longer chunks.
- Known-anchor coverage goal: learners can identify a compact Gothic word set
  while seeing manuscript and sacred-context caveats.
- Deferred high-load material: exact biblical verses, full grammar, punctuation,
  abbreviations, pronunciation reconstruction, and all alphabetic numeric uses.
