# Meitei Mayek Reading Lesson Sequence

This staged outline targets Meitei Mayek reading for practical beginner text. It
is not final learner-facing copy and requires Manipuri/Meitei review.

## Course Shape

- First-session reading win: read `ꯆꯥ`, `ꯏꯁꯤꯡ`, and `ꯃꯩꯇꯩ` as short useful or
  identity words.
- Approximate lesson count: 12 core lessons plus optional loanword cluster review.
- Structural family: LTR script with base letters, dependent vowel signs, distinct
  lonsum final consonant letters, and loanword clusters.
- Required app support beyond the current Thai runtime: language-agnostic text
  fields, final-letter metadata, combining-mark-aware segmentation, font QA, and
  reviewer-controlled romanization settings.

## Stage 1: First Decoding Wins

| Lesson | Anchor | New units  | Rule or pattern                                | Review units | Drill focus                                    |
| ------ | ------ | ---------- | ---------------------------------------------- | ------------ | ---------------------------------------------- |
| 1      | `ꯆꯥ`   | ꯆ, ꯥ       | Vowel signs modify the base letter.            | none         | Decode a compact food or drink word.           |
| 2      | `ꯏꯁꯤꯡ` | ꯏ, ꯁ, ꯤ, ꯡ | Lonsum letters represent final consonants.     | none         | Read water with a final ng letter.             |
| 3      | `ꯃꯩꯇꯩ` | ꯃ, ꯩ, ꯇ    | Identity words can introduce a diphthong sign. | ꯏ            | Decode Meitei and compare vowel signs.         |
| 4      | `ꯆꯥꯛ`  | ꯛ          | Final consonant letters are separate units.    | ꯆ, ꯥ         | Read food or rice and contrast final k and ng. |

## Coverage Notes

- Target-domain samples: classroom primer words, water and food terms, identity
  labels, family words, roads or place labels, public signs, and school labels.
- Known-unit coverage goal: by Lesson 4, learners can read base letters with aa,
  i, ei, final ng, and final k.
- Known-word coverage goal: by Lesson 8, learners can recognize tea or meal,
  water, Meitei, food or rice, house, road or land, mother, and father.
- Deferred high-load material: full alphabet order, Bengali-script comparison,
  historical orthography, loanword clusters, numerals, punctuation, and copied
  corpus examples.
