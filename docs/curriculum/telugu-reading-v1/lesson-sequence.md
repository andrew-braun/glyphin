# Telugu Reading Lesson Sequence

This staged outline is a review-ready starting point, not final learner-facing
lesson copy. It targets practical Telugu reading before full script-chart
completion.

## Course Shape

- First-session reading win: read `టీ`, `నీరు`, `కాఫీ`, and `పాలు` in cafe or
  menu contexts.
- Approximate lesson count: 15 core lessons plus late review for dense facility
  words and conjunct clusters.
- Structural family: South Indic abugida with consonant bases, inherent vowel,
  dependent vowel signs, anusvara, virama, and subjoined conjunct forms.
- Required app support beyond the current Thai runtime: language-agnostic text
  fields, teachable units inside aksharas, virama and conjunct metadata,
  script-aware fonts, and per-course progress state.

## Stage 1: First Decoding Wins

| Lesson | Anchor | New units | Rule or pattern                                                      | Review units | Drill focus                                         |
| ------ | ------ | --------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------ | --------------------------------------------------- |
| 1      | `టీ`   | ట, ఈ/ీ    | A consonant base carries an inherent vowel unless a sign changes it. | None         | Match a short cafe word to base plus vowel sign.    |
| 2      | `నీరు` | న, ర, ఉ/ు | Dependent vowel signs modify the consonant they attach to.           | ఈ/ీ          | Decode a survival word with long and short vowels.  |
| 3      | `కాఫీ` | క, ఆ/ా, ఫ | Loanwords can still teach regular vowel-sign decoding.               | ఈ/ీ          | Chunk a cafe word into consonant-vowel frames.      |
| 4      | `పాలు` | ప, ల      | Long vowel signs and final vowel signs recur in menu words.          | ఆ/ా, ఉ/ు     | Build short food and drink words from known frames. |

## Stage 2: Core Menus And Transit

| Lesson | Anchor   | New units         | Rule or pattern                                                        | Review units   | Drill focus                                           |
| ------ | -------- | ----------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------- | ----------------------------------------------------- |
| 5      | `అన్నం`  | అ, ం, doubled న్న | Virama-driven doubling creates a visible cluster inside an akshara.    | న              | Read a food staple while introducing cluster caution. |
| 6      | `ధర`     | ధ                 | Telugu distinguishes aspirated and unaspirated consonants in spelling. | ర              | Read price labels and compare త or ద families later.  |
| 7      | `దుకాణం` | ద, ణ              | Longer shop words can be chunked into known syllables.                 | క, ఆ/ా, ఉ/ు, ం | Decode shop signage and anusvara endings.             |
| 8      | `రైలు`   | ఐ/ై               | The ai vowel sign is a single teachable unit on a consonant base.      | ర, ల, ఉ/ు      | Read a transit word with a new sign.                  |

## Stage 3: Virama, Loanwords, And Facilities

| Lesson | Anchor    | New units          | Rule or pattern                                                       | Review units | Drill focus                                              |
| ------ | --------- | ------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------ | -------------------------------------------------------- |
| 9      | `బస్`     | బ, స, virama `్`   | Virama suppresses the inherent vowel and appears in loanword endings. | None         | Contrast open `స` with closed `స్`.                      |
| 10     | `హోటల్`   | హ, ఓ/ో, final ల్   | Final closed consonants need visible and typed-form review.           | ట, ల, virama | Read hotel signs without overgeneralizing loan spelling. |
| 11     | `మందు`    | మ                  | Nasal-plus-stop sequences need reviewer-approved segmentation.        | న, ద, ఉ/ు    | Decode a medicine word and prepare pharmacy vocabulary.  |
| 12     | `ప్రవేశం` | ప్ర conjunct, వ, శ | Conjuncts should enter through high-value sign words.                 | ర, ం         | Identify entrance signs and the pr cluster.              |

## Coverage Notes

- Target-domain samples: tea stalls, restaurant menus, shop signs, bus and rail
  labels, hotel signs, pharmacy signs, hospital signs, and entrance or exit
  signage.
- Known-grapheme or known-unit coverage goal: by Lesson 8, cover common vowel
  signs and high-payoff consonants for food and transit; by Lesson 12, add
  virama, anusvara, and the first practical conjuncts.
- Known-word or known-anchor coverage goal: learner recognizes at least seven
  useful words by Lesson 8 and a compact facility set by Lesson 15.
- Deferred high-load material: full vowel inventory, full varga chart,
  repha-style behavior, rare conjuncts, numerals, handwriting, production
  spelling, and grammar.
