# Khmer Reading Lesson Sequence

This staged outline is a review-ready starting point, not final learner-facing
lesson copy. It targets practical Khmer reading before full consonant-series or
dictionary segmentation mastery.

## Course Shape

- First-session reading win: read `ទឹក`, `បាយ`, and `តែ` in menu and cafe
  contexts.
- Approximate lesson count: 15 core lessons plus late public-facility review.
- Structural family: Khmer abugida with consonant-series vowel values,
  dependent vowels before, above, below, after, or around bases, coeng subscript
  consonants, final consonants, and phrase-level spaces.
- Required app support beyond the current Thai runtime: language-agnostic text
  fields, dictionary segmentation, orthographic-syllable segments, vowel-position
  metadata, coeng cluster metadata, script-aware fonts, and course-aware
  progress state.

## Stage 1: First Decoding Wins

| Lesson | Anchor | New units | Rule or pattern                                                 | Review units | Drill focus                                                   |
| ------ | ------ | --------- | --------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1      | `ទឹក`  | ទ, ឹ, ក   | Vowel signs can sit above or inside a visible syllable cluster. | None         | Read water and scan the whole cluster before sounding it out. |
| 2      | `បាយ`  | ប, ា, យ   | A right-side vowel sign modifies the consonant base.            | ក            | Decode a high-frequency food word by base-plus-vowel chunks.  |
| 3      | `តែ`   | ត, ែ      | Some vowel signs appear before the base but are read with it.   | ទ, ក         | Practice visual order versus reading order.                   |
| 4      | `សាលា` | ស, ល      | Repeated aa signs can make a transparent sign word.             | ា            | Read school or hall signage with repeated vowel frames.       |

## Coverage Notes

- Target-domain samples: menus, cafes, markets, shop signs, road signs,
  school labels, hotel signs, entrance and exit signs, toilets, hospitals, and
  pharmacy labels.
- Known-grapheme or known-unit coverage goal: by Lesson 4, cover early base
  consonants and common vowel positions; by Lesson 8, add coeng awareness and
  first wayfinding clusters; by Lesson 15, cover dense facility chunking.
- Known-word or known-anchor coverage goal: learner reads at least six useful
  menu, market, and wayfinding words by Lesson 8 and a compact facility set by
  Lesson 15.
- Deferred high-load material: full consonant-series chart, independent vowel
  inventory, Khmer numerals, full coeng inventory, Sanskrit and Pali spelling
  layers, handwriting, production spelling, and grammar.
