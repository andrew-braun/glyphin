# Armenian Reading Lesson Sequence

This staged outline targets modern Eastern Armenian reading for practical signs,
menus, labels, and cultural-site text. It is not final learner-facing copy.

## Course Shape

- First-session reading win: read `ջուր`, `թեյ`, and `հաց` as useful menu words.
- Approximate lesson count: 14 core lessons plus optional phrase review.
- Structural family: alphabet with case pairs, Armenian punctuation, aspirated
  consonant contrasts, the `ու` digraph, and the `և` ligature.
- Required app support beyond the current Thai runtime: language-agnostic text
  fields, case-pair metadata, multi-letter pedagogical units, script-aware
  punctuation, and per-course progress storage.

## Stage 1: First Decoding Wins

| Lesson | Anchor | New units       | Rule or pattern                                           | Review units | Drill focus                                         |
| ------ | ------ | --------------- | --------------------------------------------------------- | ------------ | --------------------------------------------------- |
| 1      | `ջուր` | Ջ ջ, Ու ու, Ր ր | `ու` is taught as one /u/ reading unit.                   | none         | Decode a short survival word by chunking a digraph. |
| 2      | `թեյ`  | Թ թ, Ե ե, Յ յ   | Some Armenian letters are aspirated consonants.           | Ր ր          | Read a short cafe word and notice final յ.          |
| 3      | `հաց`  | Հ հ, Ա ա, Ց ց   | Armenian has distinct affricate letters.                  | Թ թ, Ե ե     | Read a compact food word with a new consonant.      |
| 4      | `կաթ`  | Կ կ, Ա review   | Plain and aspirated stops should be contrasted gradually. | Թ թ, Ա ա     | Contrast կ and թ in short menu words.               |

## Stage 2: Menus And Transit

| Lesson | Anchor    | New units       | Rule or pattern                                         | Review units  | Drill focus                                         |
| ------ | --------- | --------------- | ------------------------------------------------------- | ------------- | --------------------------------------------------- |
| 5      | `սուրճ`   | Ս ս, Ճ ճ        | Longer words can still contain familiar chunks.         | Ու ու, Ր ր    | Read coffee and identify `ու` inside a longer word. |
| 6      | `մետրո`   | Մ մ, Տ տ, Օ օ   | Loanwords can give real transit wins.                   | Ե ե, Ր ր      | Decode metro signage and compare letter shapes.     |
| 7      | `կանգառ`  | Ն ն, Գ գ        | Repeated vowels and nasals help chunk sign words.       | Կ կ, Ա ա, Ր ր | Read a transit stop word by syllable-like chunks.   |
| 8      | `ճաշարան` | Շ շ, Ն ն review | Restaurant words justify affricate and sibilant review. | Ճ ճ, Ա ա, Ր ր | Identify restaurant signs and repeated ա.           |

## Stage 3: Public Facilities And Storefronts

| Lesson | Anchor     | New units            | Rule or pattern                                        | Review units    | Drill focus                                       |
| ------ | ---------- | -------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------ | --------------- | ------------------------------------------------- |
| 9      | `ելք`      | Լ լ, Ք ք             | Short public-sign words can carry high survival value. | Ե ե             | Read exit signs and add aspirated ք.              |
| 10     | `մուտք`    | Մ մ review, Տք chunk | Entrance and exit form a sign pair.                    | Ու ու, Ք ք      | Pair entrance and exit signs in drills.           |
| 11     | `բաց`      | Բ բ                  | Storefront words are short and memorable.              | Ա ա, Ց ց        | Read open signs and contrast with closed.         |
| 12     | `փակ`      | Փ փ                  | Փ is the aspirated partner in the p family.            | Կ կ, Ա ա        | Pair open and closed storefront words.            |
| 13     | `գին`      | Ի ի                  | Price labels use compact common words.                 | Գ գ, Ն ն        | Read shopping labels and menu price prompts.      |
| 14     | `դեղատուն` | Դ դ, Ղ ղ             | Facility words can be chunked after core letters.      | Տ տ, Ու ու, Ն ն | Decode pharmacy as a late public-facility anchor. |

## Optional Review

- Add `զուգարան`, `հյուրանոց`, `թանգարան`, and `բարև` after core sign words.
- Add Armenian punctuation and uppercase sign variants after lowercase reading is
  stable.
- Add dialect notes only as reviewer-approved pronunciation metadata.

## Coverage Notes

- Target-domain samples: menus, cafe boards, metro labels, stop signs, entrance
  and exit signs, storefront signs, price labels, pharmacies, restrooms, hotels,
  markets, and museum labels.
- Known-unit coverage goal: by Lesson 8, learners can decode high-value food and
  transit anchors and recognize the `ու` digraph.
- Known-word coverage goal: by Lesson 12, learners should recognize water, tea,
  bread, coffee, metro, stop, restaurant, exit, entrance, open, and closed.
- Deferred high-load material: full alphabet order, handwriting, production
  spelling, Western Armenian pronunciation differences, full punctuation, and
  exhaustive aspirated consonant contrast drills.
