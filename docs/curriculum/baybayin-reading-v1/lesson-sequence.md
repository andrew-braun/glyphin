# Baybayin Reading Lesson Sequence

This staged outline targets Baybayin reading for Tagalog heritage and cultural
literacy. It is not final learner-facing copy and requires review of historic
versus modern final-consonant notation.

## Course Shape

- First-session reading win: read `ᜊᜊᜌᜒ`, `ᜎᜎᜃᜒ`, and selected open-syllable chunks
  before introducing final consonants.
- Approximate lesson count: 12 core lessons plus final-consonant policy review.
- Structural family: LTR abugida with inherent `a`, dependent i/e and u/o marks,
  and optional modern final-consonant notation.
- Required app support beyond the current Thai runtime: language-agnostic text
  fields, abugida-aware segmentation, dependent mark rendering, virama or
  pamudpod policy metadata, and cultural-revival context notes.

## Coverage Notes

- Target-domain samples: heritage labels, names, cultural text, classroom
  primers, simple everyday words, and modern revival examples.
- Known-unit coverage goal: by Lesson 4, learners can read open syllables and
  understand why final consonant notation needs an explicit course policy.
- Known-word coverage goal: by Lesson 8, learners can recognize woman, man,
  house, thank you, water, open, day, and a common linker.
- Deferred high-load material: historic spelling variants, final consonant policy
  beyond the chosen convention, regional scripts, Kulitan comparison, handwriting,
  and copied community examples.
