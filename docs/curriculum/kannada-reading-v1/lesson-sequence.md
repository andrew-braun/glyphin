# Kannada Reading Lesson Sequence

This staged outline is a review-ready starting point, not final learner-facing
lesson copy. It targets practical Kannada reading before full script-chart
completion.

## Course Shape

- First-session reading win: read `ನೀರು`, `ಕಾಫಿ`, `ಚಹಾ`, and `ಹಾಲು` in cafe or
  menu contexts.
- Approximate lesson count: 15 core lessons plus late review for dense facility
  words and conjunct clusters.
- Structural family: South Indic abugida with consonant bases, inherent vowel,
  dependent vowel signs, anusvara, virama, and ottakshara subjoined forms.
- Required app support beyond the current Thai runtime: language-agnostic text
  fields, teachable units inside aksharas, virama and ottakshara metadata,
  script-aware fonts, and per-course progress state.

## Stage 1: First Decoding Wins

| Lesson | Anchor | New units      | Rule or pattern                                                      | Review units | Drill focus                                         |
| ------ | ------ | -------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------ | --------------------------------------------------- |
| 1      | `ನೀರು` | ನ, ಈ/ೀ, ರ, ಉ/ು | A consonant base carries an inherent vowel unless a sign changes it. | None         | Decode a survival word with long and short vowels.  |
| 2      | `ಕಾಫಿ` | ಕ, ಆ/ಾ, ಫ, ಇ/ಿ | Dependent vowel signs modify the consonant they attach to.           | ಈ/ೀ          | Chunk a cafe word into consonant-vowel frames.      |
| 3      | `ಚಹಾ`  | ಚ, ಹ           | Loan and everyday cafe words can still teach regular frames.         | ಆ/ಾ          | Read a short word with two clear syllables.         |
| 4      | `ಹಾಲು` | ಲ              | Long vowel signs and final vowel signs recur in menu words.          | ಹ, ಆ/ಾ, ಉ/ು  | Build short food and drink words from known frames. |

## Stage 2: Core Menus And Transit

| Lesson | Anchor  | New units      | Rule or pattern                                                     | Review units | Drill focus                                           |
| ------ | ------- | -------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------ | ----------------------------------------------------- |
| 5      | `ಅನ್ನ`  | ಅ, doubled ನ್ನ | Virama-driven doubling creates a visible cluster inside an akshara. | ನ            | Read a food staple while introducing cluster caution. |
| 6      | `ಬೆಲೆ`  | ಬ, ಎ/ೆ         | Repeated vowel signs can make a compact shopping word.              | ಲ            | Read price labels and compare known frames.           |
| 7      | `ಅಂಗಡಿ` | ಂ, ಗ, ಡ        | Anusvara marks a nasal element before another consonant.            | ಅ, ಇ/ಿ       | Decode shop signage and flag retroflex contrast.      |
| 8      | `ರೈಲು`  | ಐ/ೈ            | The ai vowel sign is a single teachable unit on a consonant base.   | ರ, ಲ, ಉ/ು    | Read a transit word with a new sign.                  |

## Stage 3: Virama, Loanwords, And Facilities

| Lesson | Anchor   | New units          | Rule or pattern                                                       | Review units | Drill focus                                              |
| ------ | -------- | ------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------ | -------------------------------------------------------- |
| 9      | `ಬಸ್`    | ಸ, virama `್`      | Virama suppresses the inherent vowel and appears in loanword endings. | ಬ            | Contrast open `ಸ` with closed `ಸ್`.                      |
| 10     | `ಹೋಟೆಲ್` | ಓ/ೋ, ಟ, final ಲ್   | Final closed consonants need visible and typed-form review.           | ಹ, ಲ, virama | Read hotel signs without overgeneralizing loan spelling. |
| 11     | `ಔಷಧಿ`   | ಔ/ೌ, ಷ, ಧ          | Less common signs can enter through high-value facility words.        | ಇ/ಿ          | Recognize a medicine word and defer full chart coverage. |
| 12     | `ಪ್ರವೇಶ` | ಪ್ರ conjunct, ವ, ಶ | Conjuncts should enter through high-value sign words.                 | ರ            | Identify entrance signs and the pr ottakshara pattern.   |

## Stage 4: Dense Public-Facility Text

| Lesson | Anchor     | New units            | Rule or pattern                                                       | Review units | Drill focus                                                |
| ------ | ---------- | -------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------- |
| 13     | `ನಿರ್ಗಮನ`  | ರ್ಗ cluster          | Exit words can be chunked after virama and conjunct basics.           | ನ, ರ, ಗ, ಮ   | Recognize exit signs by known chunks and shape.            |
| 14     | `ಆಸ್ಪತ್ರೆ` | ಸ್ಪ and ತ್ರ clusters | Facility words often contain clusters but remain decodable in chunks. | ಆ/ಾ, ಪ, ತ    | Read hospital signage.                                     |
| 15     | `ಶೌಚಾಲಯ`   | ಶ, ಔ/ೌ review        | Toilet signage is useful but too dense for early lessons.             | ಚ, ಹ, ಲ      | Decode a late facility anchor after core signs are stable. |

## Coverage Notes

- Target-domain samples: cafes, restaurant menus, shop signs, bus and rail
  labels, hotel signs, medicine labels, hospital signs, and entrance or exit
  signage.
- Known-grapheme or known-unit coverage goal: by Lesson 8, cover common vowel
  signs and high-payoff consonants for food and transit; by Lesson 12, add
  virama, anusvara, and the first practical ottakshara forms.
- Known-word or known-anchor coverage goal: learner recognizes at least seven
  useful words by Lesson 8 and a compact facility set by Lesson 15.
- Deferred high-load material: full vowel inventory, full varga chart,
  rarely used signs, dense conjuncts, numerals, handwriting, production spelling,
  and grammar.
