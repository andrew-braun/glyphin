# Old Persian Cuneiform Reading Lesson Sequence

This staged outline targets beginner recognition of Old Persian cuneiform in
royal-inscription and museum contexts. It needs Old Iranian and epigraphic
review before any learner-facing publication.

## Course Shape

- First-session reading win: recognize `𐏐`, `𐎭𐎠𐎼𐎹𐎺𐎢𐏁`, and the start of
  `𐎧𐏁𐎠𐎹𐎰𐎡𐎹` in reviewed royal-inscription contexts.
- Approximate lesson count: 12 lessons plus optional royal formula review.
- Structural family: encoded left-to-right historic cuneiform with sign values,
  formulaic royal vocabulary, and a visible word divider.
- Required app support beyond the current Thai runtime: SMP font support,
  transliteration fields, inscription provenance, word-divider drills, and
  specialist review status.

## Coverage Notes

- Target-domain samples: royal inscriptions, museum labels, royal names and
  titles, word-divider exercises, and transliteration workbenches.
- Known-unit coverage goal: by Lesson 6, learners recognize the word divider,
  Darius name chunks, the king title chunk, and Parsa.
- Known-anchor coverage goal: by Lesson 12, learners can identify a compact set
  of recurring royal-inscription terms without full grammar claims.
- Deferred high-load material: full royal formulae, syntax, exact edition text,
  phonological reconstruction, damaged inscriptions, and unreviewed translations.
