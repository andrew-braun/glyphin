# Old Persian Cuneiform Reading Curriculum Notes

This durable course note records the v1 boundary and bootstrap decisions for an
Old Persian cuneiform reading course.

## Course Boundary

- Language/script: Old Persian in Old Persian cuneiform, `peo-Xpeo`, ISO 15924
  `Xpeo`.
- Target learner: museum visitor, ancient Iran learner, or historical-language
  beginner who wants to recognize royal-inscription signs and names.
- Target domains: royal inscriptions, museum labels, royal names and titles,
  word-divider practice, and transliteration workbenches.
- In scope for v1: encoded signs, word divider, Darius and royal-title chunks,
  selected formulaic words, and reviewed transliteration labels.
- Out of scope for v1: full Old Persian grammar, complete royal inscriptions,
  translation training, damaged-source restoration, and production writing.

## Sequencing Rationale

- Frequency sources: Unicode metadata, Achaemenid inscription references, TITUS
  scoring evidence, font checks, and app-authored examples for shipped content.
- First-session decoding target: the word divider plus Darius and royal-title
  chunks because those are visible and memorable in museum contexts.
- Stage 1 goal: make inscription segmentation and sign-by-sign reading concrete.
- Stage 2 goal: add royal title, place name, and sacred-name anchors.
- Stage 3 goal: add formulaic vocabulary only after sign inventory is stable.

## Script Notes

- Old Persian cuneiform is left-to-right and includes a visible word divider,
  which is an excellent early segmentation anchor.
- The corpus is small and heavily skewed toward royal Achaemenid inscriptions.
- Pronunciation and transliteration should be explicitly labeled and reviewed.
- SMP font support must be verified before runtime lessons ship.

## Validation Notes

- Segmentation review: verify sign sequences, word-divider treatment,
  transliteration, and source provenance.
- Pronunciation review: confirm all romanized values and avoid unsupported audio
  claims.
- Cultural review: handle royal and sacred terms respectfully and historically.
- License review: keep exact inscription text and third-party translations out
  of shipped content until rights are approved.
