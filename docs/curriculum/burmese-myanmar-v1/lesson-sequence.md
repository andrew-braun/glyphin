# Burmese Myanmar Reading Lesson Sequence

This staged outline is a review-ready starting point, not final learner-facing
lesson copy. It targets practical Burmese reading in the Myanmar script before
full orthographic or tone-system mastery.

## Course Shape

- First-session reading win: read `ရေ`, `ဆိုင်`, and `ဈေး` in survival, shop,
  and market contexts.
- Approximate lesson count: 15 core lessons plus late tea-shop and transit
  review.
- Structural family: Myanmar-script abugida with inherent vowel behavior,
  dependent vowels, medials, asat, anusvara, tone marks, syllable-final signs,
  and shaping-sensitive clusters.
- Required app support beyond the current Thai runtime: language-agnostic text
  fields, dictionary and syllable segmentation, asat and medial metadata,
  tone-mark metadata, no-split display spans, Myanmar font QA, and course-aware
  progress state.

## Stage 1: First Decoding Wins

| Lesson | Anchor  | New units   | Rule or pattern                                                       | Review units | Drill focus                                                |
| ------ | ------- | ----------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1      | `ရေ`    | ရ, ေ        | The e vowel sign appears before the base but belongs to the syllable. | None         | Read water and practice visual order versus reading order. |
| 2      | `ဆိုင်` | ဆ, ိ, ု, င် | Vowel signs and final marks combine inside one orthographic syllable. | ရ, ေ         | Decode a shop sign as a whole syllable frame.              |
| 3      | `ဈေး`   | ဈ, း        | Tone-like marks can be part of high-value market words.               | ေ            | Read market and price signs with mark awareness.           |
| 4      | `လမ်း`  | လ, မ, း     | Final consonants and tone marks shape compact wayfinding words.       | င်           | Read road signs by syllable chunks.                        |

## Stage 2: Food And Familiar Loanwords

| Lesson | Anchor   | New units    | Rule or pattern                                                   | Review units | Drill focus                                                     |
| ------ | -------- | ------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 5      | `ထမင်း`  | ထ, ံ         | Nasal and tone signs belong to the syllable frame.                | မ, း         | Read rice or meal as a core food word.                          |
| 6      | `ကော်ဖီ` | က, ာ, ဖ, ီ   | Familiar loanwords still require Myanmar vowel placement.         | ေ            | Read coffee and identify shaped vowel signs.                    |
| 7      | `ဟိုတယ်` | ဟ, ို, တ, ယ် | Hotel signs combine loanword chunks with final asat.              | ာ, ေ         | Decode lodging signs without overgeneralizing English spelling. |
| 8      | `ပိတ်`   | ပ, တ်        | Asat suppresses or closes the inherent vowel in final consonants. | ိ            | Read closed signs and compare open syllables.                   |

## Stage 4: Dense Utility Words

| Lesson | Anchor      | New units     | Rule or pattern                                                            | Review units | Drill focus                                           |
| ------ | ----------- | ------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------ | ----------------------------------------------------- |
| 13     | `အိမ်သာ`    | အ, အိမ် chunk | Toilet signage introduces carrier and asat-heavy chunks.                   | သ, ာ         | Decode a high-utility facility word.                  |

## Coverage Notes

- Target-domain samples: tea shops, menus, shop signs, markets, price labels,
  road signs, bus or vehicle labels, hotels, hospitals, toilets, and open or
  closed signs.
- Known-grapheme or known-unit coverage goal: by Lesson 4, cover e vowel,
  market and road words, final marks, and first tone marks; by Lesson 12, add
  asat, common vowel signs, enter and exit signs, and first facility chunks.
- Known-word or known-anchor coverage goal: learner reads at least six practical
  survival, shop, market, and lodging words by Lesson 8 and a compact public-sign
  set by Lesson 15.
- Deferred high-load material: full consonant chart, complete medial system,
  kinzi, Pali and Sanskrit loan spelling, numerals, handwriting, production
  spelling, full tone analysis, and grammar.
