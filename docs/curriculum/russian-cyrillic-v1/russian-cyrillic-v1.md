# Russian Cyrillic Reading Curriculum Notes

Russian Cyrillic Reading v1 is a practical decoding course for modern Russian
environmental print. It favors immediate signage and menu wins over exhaustive
alphabet coverage.

## Course Boundary

- Language/script: Russian in Cyrillic, `ru-Cyrl`, ISO 15924 `Cyrl`.
- Target learner: English-literate traveler or cultural learner reading menus,
  transit signs, public facilities, and labels.
- Target domains: food and cafe menus, transit signage, public facilities,
  everyday labels, and hotel/lodging.
- In scope for v1: modern Russian letters, case-pair recognition, high-risk
  Latin confusables, soft-vowel introduction, hard/soft sign awareness, and
  optional stress marks for pronunciation.
- Out of scope for v1: Old Church Slavonic, pre-1918 orthography, handwriting,
  declension, conjugation, transliteration standards, and production spelling.

## Sequencing Rationale

- Start with `вода`, `кофе`, and `чай` because they are short and high-utility.
- Introduce Latin-lookalike traps early: В is v, Н is n, Р is r, С is s, Х is kh.
- Pair `вход` and `выход` to make `Ы` meaningful instead of isolated.
- Keep soft-vowel and palatalization details light in v1; they affect
  pronunciation more than basic sign reading.

## Validation Notes

- Segmentation review: Russian is whitespace-delimited, but stress-mark overlays
  and soft/hard signs need metadata beyond simple code-point lists.
- Pronunciation review: native-speaker review is required for stress hints and
  soft-vowel notes.
- Cultural review: confirm anchor naturalness in current signage and traveler
  contexts.
- License review: keep Wiktionary, OpenStreetMap, and corpus-derived examples
  discovery/scoring-only until attribution obligations are approved.
