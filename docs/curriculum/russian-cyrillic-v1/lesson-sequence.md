# Russian Cyrillic Reading Lesson Sequence

This v1 teaches practical recognition of modern Russian Cyrillic in menus,
transit signs, public facilities, and everyday labels. It is a reading course,
not a grammar or handwriting course.

## Course Shape

- First-session reading win: read `вода`, `кофе`, and `чай` in cafe/menu
  contexts.
- Approximate lesson count: 16 core lessons plus optional consolidation.
- Structural family: Cyrillic alphabet with case pairs, Latin-lookalike
  confusables, and orthographic modifiers.
- Required app support beyond the current Thai runtime: case-pair metadata,
  confusable drills, optional stress marks, and modifier-style handling for
  soft and hard signs.

## Stage 1: Cafe And Transit Wins

| Lesson | Anchor  | New units  | Rule or pattern                                               | Review units | Drill focus                  |
| ------ | ------- | ---------- | ------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------ | ---------------------------- |
| 1      | `вода`  | В, О, Д, А | Cyrillic letters may look familiar but map to Russian sounds. | none         | Read a survival drink word.  |
| 2      | `кофе`  | К, Ф, Е    | К is k and Ф is f.                                            | О, А         | Decode a familiar cafe word. |
| 3      | `чай`   | Ч, Й       | Ч is ch and Й marks a short i/y sound.                        | А            | Read a short drink word.     |
| 4      | `метро` | М, Т, Р    | Р looks like Latin P but sounds r.                            | Е, О         | Read transit signage.        |

## Stage 2: Food And Sign Words

| Lesson | Anchor     | New units | Rule or pattern                             | Review units     | Drill focus                           |
| ------ | ---------- | --------- | ------------------------------------------- | ---------------- | ------------------------------------- |
| 5      | `молоко`   | Л         | Repeated vowels make longer words readable. | М, О, К          | Decode repeated-letter patterns.      |
| 6      | `салат`    | С         | С looks like Latin C but sounds s.          | А, Л, Т          | Read menu loanwords.                  |
| 7      | `ресторан` | Н         | Н looks like Latin H but sounds n.          | Р, Е, С, Т, О, А | Decode a longer restaurant sign word. |
| 8      | `мясо`     | Я         | Я is a soft-vowel letter with ya value.     | М, С, О          | Introduce soft-vowel behavior.        |
| 9      | `такси`    | И         | И is i and appears in familiar loanwords.   | Т, А, К, С       | Read taxi and transit labels.         |
| 10     | `хлеб`     | Х, Б      | Х is kh and Б is b.                         | Л, Е             | Contrast non-Latin consonants.        |

## Stage 3: Facilities And Modifiers

| Lesson | Anchor   | New units | Rule or pattern                               | Review units | Drill focus                    |
| ------ | -------- | --------- | --------------------------------------------- | ------------ | ------------------------------ |
| 11     | `меню`   | Ю         | Ю is a soft-vowel letter with yu value.       | М, Е, Н      | Read menu signage.             |
| 12     | `вход`   | Х review  | Entrance and exit form a sign pair.           | В, О, Д      | Read public entrance signs.    |
| 13     | `выход`  | Ы         | Ы is a Russian-specific hard i-like vowel.    | В, Х, О, Д   | Contrast вход and выход.       |
| 14     | `туалет` | У         | Longer facility words can be read by chunks.  | Т, А, Л, Е   | Read restroom signs.           |
| 15     | `цена`   | Ц         | Ц is ts and often appears in price words.     | Е, Н, А      | Read shopping and menu labels. |
| 16     | `аптека` | П         | Public facility words reinforce core letters. | А, Т, Е, К   | Read pharmacy labels.          |

## Optional Consolidation

- Add `Ш`, `Ж`, `Ь`, and `Ъ` after the core sign-reading path.
- Add uppercase sign drills for В/Н/Р/С and Latin-lookalike traps.
- Add optional stress-mark overlays for pronunciation notes without changing the
  canonical spelling.

## Coverage Notes

- Target-domain samples: cafe boards, restaurant menus, metro signs, entrance
  and exit signs, restroom labels, price labels, pharmacy signs, and hotel desk
  labels.
- Known-unit coverage goal: by Lesson 13, learners can distinguish core
  direction and facility signs and avoid the highest-risk Latin lookalikes.
- Deferred high-load material: full Russian phonology, declension, handwriting,
  production spelling, and advanced palatalization.
