# Ugaritic Reading Lesson Sequence

This staged outline targets beginner Ugaritic alphabetic-cuneiform recognition
for tablets, museum labels, and transliteration workbenches. It requires
Northwest Semitic specialist review before learner-facing publication.

## Course Shape

- First-session reading win: recognize `𐎟`, `𐎎𐎍𐎋`, and `𐎊𐎎` as reviewed
  tablet-reading anchors.
- Approximate lesson count: 12 lessons plus optional mythological-name review.
- Structural family: left-to-right alphabetic cuneiform with word dividers,
  unvowelized Semitic words, aleph distinctions, and tablet corpus constraints.
- Required app support beyond the current Thai runtime: transliteration fields,
  word-divider drills, Semitic consonant labels, sacred-name context, and Ugaritic
  font fallback.

## Coverage Notes

- Target-domain samples: alphabetic tablets, mythological text excerpts in
  transliteration, royal and city names, museum labels, and sign charts.
- Known-unit coverage goal: by Lesson 8, learners recognize the divider, core
  consonants, aleph, ayin, and several short high-salience words.
- Known-anchor coverage goal: learners can decode a compact set of tablet words
  while seeing vowels and interpretation as reviewed layers.
- Deferred high-load material: full mythological passages, vocalization,
  grammatical parsing, exact corpus text, rare consonants, and damaged tablets.
