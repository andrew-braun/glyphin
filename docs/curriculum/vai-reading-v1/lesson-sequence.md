# Vai Reading Lesson Sequence

This staged outline is a review-ready starting point, not final learner-facing
lesson copy. It uses Unicode Vai signs where safe and marks word-level anchors as
reviewer-gated when corpus confidence is low.

## Course Shape

- First-session reading win: read `ꕙꔤ` as a script or language identity candidate
  after reviewer confirmation.
- Approximate lesson count: 12 core lessons plus reviewer-selected word sets.
- Structural family: left-to-right syllabary with a large encoded inventory and
  limited open frequency data.
- Required app support beyond the current Thai runtime: syllabary inventory
  metadata, font coverage, reviewer-selected anchors, language-specific
  pronunciation notes, and source-confidence flags.

## Coverage Notes

- Target-domain samples: script identity text, dictionary headwords, community
  literacy materials, cultural-site labels, and short app-authored labels.
- Known-unit coverage goal: establish a small set of visually distinct signs and
  one approved identity anchor before wider vocabulary.
- Known-anchor coverage goal: replace placeholder sequences with reviewer-supplied
  greeting, water, and public-label anchors before implementation.
- Deferred high-load material: full syllabary chart, nasalized sign inventory,
  large corpus scoring, historical variants, handwriting, and copied examples.
