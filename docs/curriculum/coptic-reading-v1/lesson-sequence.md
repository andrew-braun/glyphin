# Coptic Reading Lesson Sequence

This staged outline targets beginner Coptic script reading for liturgical,
manuscript, museum, and dictionary contexts. It is a historical-language reading
course and needs specialist review before learner-facing publication.

## Course Shape

- First-session reading win: read `ⲙⲟⲟⲩ`, `ⲣⲱⲙⲉ`, and `ⲡⲉ` as real Coptic words.
- Approximate lesson count: 12 core lessons plus optional liturgical review.
- Structural family: alphabetic script with Greek-derived and Coptic-specific
  letters, edition-dependent marks, and dialect-sensitive pronunciation.
- Required app support beyond the current Thai runtime: historic-language course
  metadata, dialect labels, combining-mark handling, Coptic font support,
  manuscript/source edition notes, and non-travel target domains.

## Optional Review

- Add Coptic-specific letters such as `ϣ`, `ϥ`, `ϩ`, `ϫ`, `ϭ`, and `ϯ` when
  reviewed anchors justify them.
- Add supralinear mark recognition only after edition and dialect policy are
  settled.
- Add dialect labels before mixing Sahidic and Bohairic examples.

## Coverage Notes

- Target-domain samples: printed liturgical texts, learner grammars, dictionary
  headwords, museum labels, manuscript transcriptions, and hymn/service books.
- Known-unit coverage goal: by Lesson 8, learners can recognize the highest-use
  vowels, core consonants, short grammatical forms, and several common nouns or
  verbs.
- Known-word coverage goal: by Lesson 12, learners should recognize water,
  person, short copular forms, see, hear, eat, God, light, mother, father, and a
  late Greek loan anchor.
- Deferred high-load material: full dialect comparison, manuscript paleography,
  supralinear abbreviation systems, Coptic numerals, full grammar, and production
  pronunciation.
