# Brahmi Reading Lesson Sequence

This staged outline targets inscription-focused Brahmi recognition for museum,
edict, and donative-label contexts. It needs Brahmi and South Asian epigraphy
review before learner-facing publication.

## Course Shape

- First-session reading win: read `𑀭𑀸𑀚`, `𑀤𑀸𑀦`, and `𑀫𑀳𑀸` as reviewed
  Brahmi inscription-style words.
- Approximate lesson count: 12 lessons plus optional Ashokan-name review.
- Structural family: abugida with consonant bases, inherent vowel, vowel signs,
  virama or conjunct behavior, and inscriptional variation.
- Required app support beyond the current Thai runtime: akshara-aware
  segmentation, combining-mark drills, transliteration fields, inscription
  provenance, font fallback, and specialist-review gates.

## Coverage Notes

- Target-domain samples: Ashokan edict terms, museum inscriptions, donative
  labels, royal names and titles, and transliteration workbenches.
- Known-unit coverage goal: by Lesson 8, learners understand consonant bases,
  inherent vowel assumptions, aa and i signs, and a small set of inscription
  anchors.
- Known-anchor coverage goal: learners can identify several reviewed inscription
  words while seeing Prakrit and Sanskrit scope as unresolved.
- Deferred high-load material: regional Brahmi variants, full edict passages,
  conjunct systems, full phonology, exact inscription text, and paleography.
