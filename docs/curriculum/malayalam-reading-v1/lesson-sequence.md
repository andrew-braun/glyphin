# Malayalam Reading Lesson Sequence

This staged outline is a review-ready starting point, not final learner-facing
lesson copy. It targets practical Malayalam reading before full script-chart
completion.

## Course Shape

- First-session reading win: read `കട`, `വില`, `ചായ`, and `പാൽ` in shop or menu
  contexts.
- Approximate lesson count: 15 core lessons plus late review for dense facility
  words, chillus, and conjunct clusters.
- Structural family: South Indic abugida with consonant bases, inherent vowel,
  dependent vowel signs, anusvara, chandrakkala, chillus, and conjunct forms.
- Required app support beyond the current Thai runtime: language-agnostic text
  fields, teachable units inside aksharas, chillu and chandrakkala metadata,
  script-aware fonts, and per-course progress state.

## Stage 1: First Decoding Wins

| Lesson | Anchor | New units   | Rule or pattern                                                      | Review units | Drill focus                                     |
| ------ | ------ | ----------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------ | ----------------------------------------------- |
| 1      | `കട`   | ക, ട        | A consonant base carries an inherent vowel unless a sign changes it. | None         | Read a shop word from two open consonant bases. |
| 2      | `വില`  | വ, ഇ/ി, ല   | Dependent vowel signs modify the consonant they attach to.           | None         | Decode a price word with one vowel sign.        |
| 3      | `ചായ`  | ച, ആ/ാ, യ   | Long vowel signs recur in cafe and food words.                       | ക, ല         | Read a short cafe word with clear frames.       |
| 4      | `പാൽ`  | പ, ൽ chillu | Chillus write consonants without the inherent vowel.                 | ആ/ാ, ല       | Contrast open `ല` with chillu `ൽ`.              |

## Stage 2: Core Menus And Food

| Lesson | Anchor   | New units        | Rule or pattern                                                  | Review units   | Drill focus                                      |
| ------ | -------- | ---------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------- | ------------------------------------------------ |
| 5      | `അരി`    | അ, ര             | Independent vowels start syllables without a consonant base.     | ഇ/ി            | Read a food staple and reinforce vowel signs.    |
| 6      | `കാപ്പി` | geminate പ്പ     | Chandrakkala can form conjunct or doubled consonant shapes.      | ക, ആ/ാ, പ, ഇ/ി | Read a cafe word by chunking the geminate.       |
| 7      | `അപ്പം`  | ം anusvara       | Anusvara marks a nasal element and appears in food words.        | അ, പ്പ         | Decode a familiar food word with nasal ending.   |
| 8      | `വെള്ളം` | എ/െ, ള്ള cluster | Useful words can have high-load conjuncts and should be delayed. | വ, ം           | Read water after learners know the script model. |

## Stage 3: Transit, Loanwords, And Facilities

| Lesson | Anchor    | New units              | Rule or pattern                                                      | Review units | Drill focus                                         |
| ------ | --------- | ---------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------ | --------------------------------------------------- |
| 9      | `ബസ്`     | ബ, സ, chandrakkala `്` | Chandrakkala suppresses the inherent vowel in loanword endings.      | None         | Contrast open `സ` with closed `സ്`.                 |
| 10     | `ഹോട്ടൽ`  | ഹ, ഓ/ോ, final ൽ        | Chillu letters are common in practical signs.                        | ട, ൽ         | Read hotel signs and review chillu display.         |
| 11     | `മരുന്ന്` | മ, ന്ന് cluster        | Medicine words require geminate and final consonant review.          | ര, ഉ/ു       | Decode a pharmacy concept in chunks.                |
| 12     | `ട്രെയിൻ` | ട്ര cluster, ൻ chillu  | Some high-value transit words are loanword-heavy and visually dense. | എ/െ          | Recognize train signs as late practical vocabulary. |

## Stage 4: Dense Public-Facility Text

| Lesson | Anchor     | New units       | Rule or pattern                                                 | Review units | Drill focus                                                |
| ------ | ---------- | --------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------- |
| 13     | `പ്രവേശനം` | പ്ര conjunct, ശ | Entrance words can be chunked after chillu and conjunct basics. | വ, എ/െ, ന, ം | Identify entrance signs and the pr cluster.                |
| 14     | `പുറത്ത്`  | റ, ത്ത cluster  | Exit or outside text may use doubled consonant shapes.          | പ, ഉ/ു       | Read a high-utility sign word with cluster review.         |
| 15     | `ശൗചാലയം`  | ശ, ഔ/ൗ          | Toilet signage is useful but too dense for early lessons.       | ച, ആ/ാ, ല, യ | Decode a late facility anchor after core signs are stable. |

## Coverage Notes

- Target-domain samples: shop signs, restaurant menus, tea shops, packaged food
  labels, bus and rail labels, hotel signs, medicine labels, and entrance or exit
  signage.
- Known-grapheme or known-unit coverage goal: by Lesson 8, cover common vowel
  signs, high-payoff consonants, anusvara, and the first chandrakkala-driven
  clusters; by Lesson 12, add chillus and dense loanword handling.
- Known-word or known-anchor coverage goal: learner recognizes at least seven
  useful words by Lesson 8 and a compact facility set by Lesson 15.
- Deferred high-load material: full vowel inventory, full consonant chart,
  traditional ligature inventory, reformed-versus-traditional orthography,
  numerals, handwriting, production spelling, and grammar.
