# Deseret Reading Curriculum Notes

This durable course note records the v1 boundary and bootstrap decisions for a
Deseret alphabet reading course.

## Course Boundary

- Language/script: English in the Deseret alphabet, `en-Dsrt`, ISO 15924 `Dsrt`.
- Target learner: script enthusiast, historical-writing reader, spelling-reform
  learner, or museum visitor.
- Target domains: script identity text, phonemic English examples, historical
  context labels, learner transcription drills, and short public labels.
- In scope for v1: Unicode Deseret letters, case pairs, a chosen English
  pronunciation model, app-authored reviewed transcriptions, and font QA.
- Out of scope for v1: automatic transliteration, multiple accent models, full
  historical text reading, production spelling, and copied examples.

## Sequencing Rationale

- Frequency sources: Unicode metadata for inventory, English word frequency for
  anchor utility, historical references for discovery, and app-authored
  transcriptions for shipped content.
- First-session decoding target: `𐐔𐐯𐑆𐐯𐑉𐐯𐐻` and a short public-label anchor
  because they quickly show script identity and phonemic spelling.
- Stage 1 goal: establish sound-letter mapping and case pairs.
- Stage 2 goal: add public labels and common vowels while preserving one accent
  policy.
- Stage 3 goal: add historical context and accent-sensitive examples only after
  review.

## Script Notes

- Deseret is encoded and historically well documented but modern practical
  corpora are small.
- Case pairs are visually distinct and should be taught as part of display and
  matching.
- Deseret examples need historical framing so learners understand the script's
  context without turning the course into a religious or institutional claim.

## Validation Notes

- Segmentation review: verify each Deseret character and case pair.
- Pronunciation review: choose one accent model and confirm all transcriptions.
- Cultural review: validate historical framing and avoid unsupported claims.
- License review: keep external historical references discovery-only until reuse
  terms are approved.
