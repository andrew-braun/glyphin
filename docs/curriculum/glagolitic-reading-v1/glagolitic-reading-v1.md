# Glagolitic Reading Curriculum Notes

This durable course note records the v1 boundary and bootstrap decisions for a
Glagolitic reading course.

## Course Boundary

- Language/script: Old Church Slavonic in Glagolitic script, `cu-Glag`, ISO
  15924 `Glag`.
- Target learner: liturgical reader, medieval manuscript learner, museum visitor,
  or Slavic-studies beginner who wants to recognize Glagolitic text.
- Target domains: liturgical texts, manuscript transcriptions, alphabet prayers,
  museum and cultural sites, and transliteration workbenches.
- In scope for v1: core letters, selected alphabet-name words, yer signs, simple
  liturgical words, and reviewed transliteration labels.
- Out of scope for v1: full Old Church Slavonic grammar, manuscript paleography,
  recension comparison, exact copied liturgical passages, and production writing.

## Sequencing Rationale

- Frequency sources: Unicode metadata, Old Church Slavonic corpus references,
  manuscript discovery sources, Wiktionary for discovery-only gloss checks, font
  checks, and app-authored examples for shipped content.
- First-session decoding target: `ⰰⰸⱏ`, `ⱄⰾⱁⰲⱁ`, and `ⰴⱁⰱⱃⱁ`, because they
  are memorable and connect script identity to real words.
- Stage 1 goal: build confidence with distinctive letters and repeated vowels.
- Stage 2 goal: add liturgical and museum-relevant words with cultural context.
- Stage 3 goal: defer sacred names and complex signs until review support is
  clear.

## Script Notes

- Glagolitic is alphabetic and left-to-right in this v1 but belongs to a
  historic and liturgical language context.
- Yer signs and historic vowel letters need course-level pronunciation policy.
- Manuscript abbreviations and titlo marks should not be normalized away.
- Font support should be verified because distinctive letter forms are central to
  the learning experience.

## Validation Notes

- Segmentation review: verify Glagolitic code points, word boundaries, yer signs,
  and manuscript mark policy.
- Pronunciation review: label Old Church Slavonic pronunciation conventions and
  avoid unreviewed audio.
- Cultural review: handle liturgical words and sacred names respectfully.
- License review: keep corpus and manuscript transcriptions out of shipped
  content until reuse obligations are approved.
