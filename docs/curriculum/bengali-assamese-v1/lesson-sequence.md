# Bengali-Assamese Reading Lesson Sequence

This staged outline is Bengali-first. Assamese-specific letters and vocabulary
are noted for shared-script awareness but are not mixed into first-pass Bengali
anchors.

## Course Shape

- First-session reading win: read `চা`, `ভাত`, and `বাস` from menu and transit
  contexts.
- Approximate lesson count: 15 core lessons plus optional Assamese contrast
  notes.
- Structural family: Bengali-script abugida with inherent vowel, dependent vowel
  signs, hasanta, conjunct clusters, nasal signs, and Assamese-specific letters.
- Required app support beyond the current Thai runtime: language-agnostic text
  fields, akshara segmentation, matra visual-order metadata, conjunct metadata,
  and course notes for shared-script variants.

## Stage 1: First Decoding Wins

| Lesson | Anchor | New units | Rule or pattern                                             | Review units | Drill focus                                  |
| ------ | ------ | --------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- | ------------ | -------------------------------------------- |
| 1      | `চা`   | চ, আ/া    | Bengali consonants carry an inherent vowel unless changed.  | none         | Build a first akshara with a vowel sign.     |
| 2      | `ভাত`  | ভ, ত      | Bare consonants can carry the inherent vowel.               | আ/া          | Read a common food word by akshara chunks.   |
| 3      | `বাস`  | ব, স      | Similar-looking letters need deliberate shape practice.     | আ/া          | Decode a transit word with known vowel sign. |
| 4      | `নাম`  | ন, ম      | Compact labels still follow the same base-plus-vowel model. | আ/া          | Identify known bases in short words.         |

## Stage 2: Menus And Street Text

| Lesson | Anchor  | New units | Rule or pattern                                                | Review units | Drill focus                                 |
| ------ | ------- | --------- | -------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------ | ------------------------------------------- |
| 5      | `পানি`  | প, ই/ি    | The short-i sign appears before the base but is read after it. | ন            | Practice visual order versus reading order. |
| 6      | `দুধ`   | দ, উ/ু    | Vowel signs can attach below or after the consonant.           | প, ন         | Sort matras by visual position.             |
| 7      | `বাজার` | জ, র      | Bengali র becomes more complex in conjuncts later.             | ব, আ/া       | Read storefront and market words.           |
| 8      | `দোকান` | ক, ও/ো    | Multi-part vowel signs still belong to one akshara.            | দ, ন         | Match vowel sign shapes to sound.           |
| 9      | `হোটেল` | হ, এ/ে, ল | Loanword signage can be chunked without full grammar.          | ও/ো          | Decode familiar public words.               |

## Stage 3: Conjuncts And Public Signs

| Lesson | Anchor   | New units | Rule or pattern                                                | Review units | Drill focus                                 |
| ------ | -------- | --------- | -------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------ | ------------------------------------------- |
| 10     | `বাংলা`  | ং         | Anusvara marks nasal sound and appears in language names.      | ব, আ/া, ল    | Read a meta word and spot nasal signs.      |
| 11     | `খোলা`   | খ         | Aspirated consonants are separate letters.                     | ও/ো, ল       | Contrast ক and খ.                           |
| 12     | `রাস্তা` | স্ + ত    | Hasanta suppresses inherent vowel and enables clusters.        | র, আ/া, স, ত | Decode one common road-sign cluster.        |
| 13     | `ট্রেন`  | ট্ + র    | Some loanword clusters are high-value transit chunks.          | এ/ে, ন       | Read train signage by cluster chunks.       |
| 14     | `স্কুল`  | স্ + ক    | Conjunct clusters should be introduced after bases are stable. | স, ক, ল      | Chunk a familiar school sign.               |
| 15     | `বন্ধ`   | ন্ + ধ    | Public signs can require conjuncts and nasal review.           | ব, ন         | Recognize open/closed signage with support. |

## Assamese Notes

- Introduce `ৰ` and `ৱ` only as comparison notes in Bengali-first v1.
- Do not use Assamese anchors as shipped lessons until an Assamese reviewer and
  separate language boundary are approved.

## Coverage Notes

- Target-domain samples: tea stalls, menus, bus signs, market and shop signs,
  school and hotel labels, public entrance/exit signs, and Bengali-script place
  names.
- Known-unit coverage goal: by Lesson 9, learners can decode common base-plus-
  matra words; by Lesson 15, learners have seen first clusters, anusvara, and
  shared-script variant notes.
- Deferred high-load material: full conjunct inventory, Bengali vowel reduction,
  all sibilant letters, candrabindu, Bengali digits, handwriting, and Assamese
  as a full separate reading course.
