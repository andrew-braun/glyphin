# Tibetan Reading Lesson Sequence

This staged outline is a review-ready starting point, not final learner-facing
lesson copy. It targets modern printed Tibetan Uchen and practical public text
before full alphabet or spelling-system mastery.

## Course Shape

- First-session reading win: read `ཇ`, `ཆུ`, and `ལམ` in tea-house, label, and
  wayfinding contexts.
- Approximate lesson count: 14 core lessons plus late stacked-cluster review.
- Structural family: Tibetan abugida with root consonants, vowel signs,
  tsheg-delimited syllables, historical prefixes and suffixes, stacked letters,
  and shad punctuation.
- Required app support beyond the current Thai runtime: language-agnostic text
  fields, tsheg-syllable segmentation, root-letter and stack metadata,
  pronunciation notes for silent or historical spelling, and Tibetan font QA.

## Coverage Notes

- Target-domain samples: tea-house menus, water labels, road and place signs,
  cultural-site labels, hotel or guest-house signs, school signs, and public
  door or direction labels.
- Known-grapheme or known-unit coverage goal: by Lesson 6, cover simple base
  letters, u vowel sign, tsheg, and reusable compounds; by Lesson 12, add vowel
  signs, written-pronounced mismatch notes, and first stacked forms.
- Known-word or known-anchor coverage goal: learner reads at least five useful
  anchors by Lesson 6 and a compact cultural-site set by Lesson 15.
- Deferred high-load material: full alphabet order, Wylie transliteration,
  Sanskrit extensions, complete prefix and suffix rules, dialect variation,
  handwriting, religious formulae, and grammar.
