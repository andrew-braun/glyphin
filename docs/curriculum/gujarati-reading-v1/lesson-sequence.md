# Gujarati Reading Lesson Sequence

This staged outline teaches modern printed Gujarati as an abugida. It starts
with practical food and transit words before adding virama clusters, nasal signs,
and rare nukta awareness.

## Course Shape

- First-session reading win: read `પાણી`, `ચા`, and `બસ` from menu and transit
  contexts.
- Approximate lesson count: 15 core lessons plus late conjunct and public-
  facility review.
- Structural family: Gujarati abugida with inherent vowel, dependent vowel
  signs, anusvara, virama, common conjuncts, and rare nukta.
- Required app support beyond the current Thai runtime: language-agnostic text
  fields, akshara segmentation, matra-position metadata, conjunct metadata,
  nasal-sign metadata, and Gujarati font checks.

## Stage 1: First Decoding Wins

| Lesson | Anchor | New units      | Rule or pattern                                                    | Review units | Drill focus                             |
| ------ | ------ | -------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------ | ------------ | --------------------------------------- |
| 1      | `પાણી` | પ, આ/ા, ણ, ઈ/ી | Consonant bases carry an inherent vowel unless a matra changes it. | none         | Build aksharas from base plus matra.    |
| 2      | `ચા`   | ચ              | The aa sign attaches after the consonant base.                     | આ/ા          | Read a compact cafe word.               |
| 3      | `બસ`   | બ, સ           | Bare consonants can carry the inherent vowel.                      | આ/ા          | Decode a transit word with known bases. |
| 4      | `નામ`  | ન, મ           | Long vowels and final bases make compact labels.                   | આ/ા          | Identify known bases in short words.    |

## Coverage Notes

- Target-domain samples: menus, tea stalls, bus signs, railway labels, markets,
  shopfronts, school labels, hospitals, and open/closed signs.
- Known-unit coverage goal: by Lesson 9, learners handle common bases and
  high-payoff matras; by Lesson 15, they have seen virama clusters, anusvara,
  and rare nukta as a representable but deferred mark.
- Deferred high-load material: full conjunct inventory, vowel reduction, all
  aspirated and retroflex contrasts, Gujarati digits, handwriting, production
  spelling, and rare nukta loan forms.
