# Shavian Reading Curriculum Notes

This durable course note records the v1 boundary and bootstrap decisions for a
Shavian reading course.

## Course Boundary

- Language/script: English in Shavian, `en-Shaw`, ISO 15924 `Shaw`.
- Target learner: script enthusiast, spelling-reform reader, or English learner
  curious about phonemic writing.
- Target domains: script identity text, phonemic English examples, learner
  transcription drills, short public labels, and constructed-script culture.
- In scope for v1: Unicode Shavian letters, a chosen English pronunciation model,
  app-authored reviewed transcriptions, compact practical labels, and font QA.
- Out of scope for v1: automatic transliteration, multiple accent models,
  production spelling, full Shavian literature, and copied community examples.

## Sequencing Rationale

- Frequency sources: Unicode metadata for inventory, English word frequency for
  anchor utility, Shavian references for discovery, and app-authored
  transcriptions for shipped content.
- First-session decoding target: `𐑞` and `𐑑𐑨𐑒𐑕𐑰` because they quickly show the
  phonemic nature of Shavian.
- Stage 1 goal: establish sound-letter mapping and a few practical words.
- Stage 2 goal: add public labels and common vowels while preserving one accent
  policy.
- Stage 3 goal: introduce dialect-sensitive examples and minimal-pair drills only
  after review.

## Script Notes

- Shavian is encoded and compact but depends on pronunciation choices rather than
  standard English spelling.
- The course needs explicit accessible names for symbols because glyphs will be
  unfamiliar to screen-reader users.
- Font fallback should be tested because Shavian support is not universal.

## Validation Notes

- Segmentation review: verify each Shavian character and word boundary.
- Pronunciation review: choose one accent model and confirm all transcriptions.
- Cultural review: frame Shavian as a constructed spelling-reform script without
  implying it replaces standard orthography for all learners.
- License review: keep external Shavian references discovery-only until reuse
  terms are approved.
